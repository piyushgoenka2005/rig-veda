HYMN CVI. AŚVINS.

उभाubhā उu नूनंnūnaṃ तदिदर्थयेथेtadidarthayethe विvi तन्वाथेtanvāthe धियोवश्त्रापसेवdhiyovaśtrāpaseva |

सध्रीचीनाsadhrīcīnā यातवेyātave परेमजीगःpremajīghaḥ सुदिनेवप्र्क्षsudinevapṛkṣa आā तंसयेथेtaṃsayethe ||
THIS very thing ye Twain hold as your object: ye weave your songs as skilful men weave garments.
That ye may come united have I waked you: ye spread out food like days of lovely weather.
10.106.1
उष्टारेवuṣṭāreva फर्वरेषुpharvareṣu शरयेथेśrayethe परायोगेवprāyogheva शवात्र्याśvātryā शासुरेथःśāsurethaḥ |

दूतेवdūteva हिhi षठोṣṭho यशसाyaśasā जनेषुjaneṣu मापmāpa सथातम्महिषेवापानातsthātammahiṣevāpānāt ||
Like two plough-bulls ye move along in traces, and seek like eager guests your bidder's banquet.
Ye are like glorious envoys mid the people: like bulls, approach the place where ye are watered.
10.106.2
साकंयुजाsākaṃyujā शकुनस्येवśakunasyeva पक्षाpakṣā पश्वेवpaśveva चित्राcitrā यजुरागमिष्टमyajurāghamiṣṭam |

अग्निरिवaghniriva देवयोर्दीदिवांसाdevayordīdivāṃsā परिज्मानेवयजथःparijmānevayajathaḥ पुरुत्राpurutrā ||
Like the two pinions of a bird, connected, like two choice animals, ye have sought our worship.
Bright as the fire the votary hath kindled, ye sacrifice in many a spot as roamers.
10.106.3
आपीāpī वोvo अस्मेasme पितरेवpitareva पुत्रोग्रेवputroghreva रुचाrucā नर्पतीवnṛpatīva तुर्यैturyai |

इर्येवiryeva पुष्ट्यैpuṣṭyai किरणेवkiraṇeva भुज्यैbhujyai शरुष्टीवानेवśruṣṭīvāneva हवमागमिष्टमhavamāghamiṣṭam ||
Ye are our kinsmen, like two sons, two fathers, strong in your splendour and like kings for conquest;
Like rays for our enjoyment, Lords to feed us, ye, like quick bearers, have obeyed our calling.
10.106.4
वंसगेवvaṃsagheva पूषर्याpūṣaryā शिम्बाताśimbātā मित्रेवmitreva रताṛtā शतराशातपन्ताśatarāśātapantā |

वाजेवोच्चाvājevoccā वयसाvayasā घर्म्येष्ठाgharmyeṣṭhā मेषेवेषासपर्याmeṣeveṣāsaparyā पुरीषाpurīṣā ||
You are like two pleasantly moving well-fed (hills) like Mitra and Varuṇa, the two bestowers of felicity, veracious, possessors of infinite wealth, happy, like two horses plump with fodder, abiding in the firmament, like two rams (are you) to be nourished with sacrificial food, to be cherished (with oblations).
10.106.5
सर्ण्येवsṛṇyeva जर्भरीjarbharī तुर्फरीतूturpharītū नैतोशेवnaitośeva तुर्फरीपर्फरीकाturpharīparpharīkā |

उदन्यजेवudanyajeva जेमनाjemanā मदेरूmaderū ताtā मेme जराय्वजरम्मरायुjarāyvajarammarāyu ||
You are like two mad elephants bending their forequarters and smiting the foe, like the two sons of Nitosa destroying (foes), and cherishing (friends); you are bright as two water-born (jewels), do you, who are victorious, (render) my decaying mortal body free from decay.
10.106.6
पज्रेवpajreva चर्चरंcarcaraṃ जारंjāraṃ मरायुmarāyu कषद्मेवार्थेषुkṣadmevārtheṣu तर्तरीथौग्राtartarīthaughrā |

रभूṛbhū नापतnāpat खरमज्राkharamajrā खरज्रुर्वायुर्नkharajrurvāyurna पर्फरत्क्षयदparpharatkṣayad रयीणामrayīṇām ||
Fierce (Aśvins), like two powerful (heroes), you enable this moving, perishable mortal (frame) to cross over to the objects (of its destination) as over water; extremely strong, like the Ṛbhus, your chariot, attained its destination swift as the wind, it pervaded (everywhere), it dispensed riches.
10.106.7
घर्मेवgharmeva मधुmadhu जठरेjaṭhare सनेरूsanerū भगेविताbhaghevitā तुर्फरीफारिवारमturpharīphārivāram |

पतरेवpatareva चचराcacarā चन्द्रनिर्णिंcandranirṇiṃ मनर्ङगामनन्याmanaṛṅghāmananyā नna जग्मीjaghmī ||
With your bellies full of the Soma, like two saucepans, preservers of wealth, destroyers of enemies. (you are) armed with hatchets, moving like two flying (birds) with forms like the moon, attaining success through the mind, like two laudable beings, (you are) approaching (the sacrifice).
10.106.8
बर्हन्तेवbṛhanteva गम्भरेषुghambhareṣu परतिष्ठांpratiṣṭhāṃ पादेवpādeva गाधंghādhaṃ तरतेविदाथःtaratevidāthaḥ |

कर्णेवkarṇeva शासुरनुśāsuranu हिhi समराथो.अंशेवsmarātho.aṃśeva नोभजतंnobhajataṃ चित्रमप्नःcitramapnaḥ ||
Like giants, ye will find firm ground to stand on in depths, like feet for one who fords a shallow.
Like cars ye will attend to him who orders: ye Two enjoy our wondrous work as sharers.
10.106.9
आरङगरेवāraṅghareva मध्वेरयेथेmadhverayethe सारघेवsāragheva गविghavi नीचीनबारेnīcīnabāre |

कीनारेवkīnāreva सवेदमासिष्विदानाsvedamāsiṣvidānā कषामेवोर्जाkṣāmevorjā सूयवसात्सचेथेsūyavasātsacethe ||
Like toiling bees ye bring to us your honey, as bees into the hide that opens downward.
10.106.10
रध्यामṛdhyāma सतोमंstomaṃ सनुयामsanuyāma वाजमाvājamā नोno मन्त्रंmantraṃ सरथेहोपयातमsarathehopayātam |

यशोyaśo नna पक्वंpakvaṃ मधुmadhu गोष्वन्तराghoṣvantarā भूतांशोश्विनोःbhūtāṃśoaśvinoḥ काममप्राःkāmamaprāḥ ||
May we increase the laud and gain us vigour: come to our song, ye whom one chariot carries.
Filled be our kine with ripened meath like glory: Bhutamsa hath fulfilled the Aśvins' longing.
10.106.11
