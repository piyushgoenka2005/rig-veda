HYMN LXIII. VIŚVEDEVAS.

परावतोparāvato येye दिधिषन्तdidhiṣanta आप्यंāpyaṃ मनुप्रीतासोmanuprītāso जनिमाविवस्वतःjanimāvivasvataḥ |

ययातेर्येyayāterye नहुष्यस्यnahuṣyasya बर्हिषिbarhiṣi देवाdevā आसतेāsate तेधिteadhi बरुवन्तुbruvantu नःnaḥ ||
MAY they who would assume kinship from far away, Vivasvān's generations, dearly loved of men,
Even the Gods who sit upon the sacred grass of Nahuṣa's son Yayāti, bless and comfort us.
10.63.1
विश्वाviśvā हिhi वोvo नमस्यानिnamasyāni वन्द्याvandyā नामानिnāmāni देवाdevā उतuta यज्ञियानिवःyajñiyānivaḥ |

येye सथstha जाताjātā अदितेरब्ध्यसaditerabdhyas परिpari येye पर्थिव्यास्तेमpṛthivyāstema इहiha शरुताśrutā हवमhavam ||
For worthy of obeisance, Gods, are all your names, worthy of adoration and of sacrifice.
Ye who were born from waters, and from Aditi, and from the earth, do ye here listen to my call.
10.63.2
येभ्योyebhyo माताmātā मधुमतmadhumat पिन्वतेpinvate पयःpayaḥ पीयूषंpīyūṣaṃ दयौरदितिरद्रिबर्हाःdyauraditiradribarhāḥ |

उक्थशुष्मानukthaśuṣmān वर्षभरानvṛṣabharān सवप्नसस्तानादित्याननुsvapnasastānādityānanu मदाmadā सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
I will rejoice in these Ādityas for my weal, for whom the Mother pours forth water rich in balm,
And Dyaus the Infinite, firm as a rock, sweet milk,—Gods active, strong through lauds, whose might the Bull upholds.
10.63.3
नर्चक्षसोnṛcakṣaso अनिमिषन्तोanimiṣanto अर्हणाarhaṇā बर्हदbṛhad देवासोdevāso अम्र्तत्वमानशुःamṛtatvamānaśuḥ |

जयोतीरथाjyotīrathā अहिमायाahimāyā अनागसोanāghaso दिवोdivo वर्ष्माणंवसतेvarṣmāṇaṃvasate सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
Looking on men, ne’er slumbering, they by their deserts attained as Gods to lofty immortality.
Borne on refulgent cars, sinless, with serpents' powers, they robe them, for our welfare, in the height of heaven.
10.63.4
सम्राजोsamrājo येye सुव्र्धोsuvṛdho यज्ञमाययुरपरिह्व्र्ताyajñamāyayuraparihvṛtā दधिरेdadhire दिविक्षयमdivikṣayam |

तानाtānā विवासvivāsa नमसाnamasā सुव्र्क्तिभिर्महोsuvṛktibhirmaho आदित्यानदितिंādityānaditiṃ सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
Great Kings who bless us, who have come to sacrifice, who, ne’er assailed, have set their mansion in the sky,—
These I invite with adoration and with hymns, mighty Ādityas, Aditi, for happiness.
10.63.5
कोko वva सतोमंstomaṃ राधतिrādhati यंyaṃ जुजोषथjujoṣatha विश्वेviśve देवासोdevāso मनुषोयतिmanuṣoyati षठनṣṭhana |

कोko वो.अध्वरंvo.adhvaraṃ तुविजाताtuvijātā अरंaraṃ करदkarad योyo नःपर्षदत्यंहःnaḥparṣadatyaṃhaḥ सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
Who offereth to you the laud that ye accept, O ye All-Gods of Manu, many as ye are?
Who, Mighty Ones, will prepare for you the sacrifice to bear us over trouble to felicity?
10.63.6
येभ्योyebhyo होत्रांhotrāṃ परथमामायेजेprathamāmāyeje मनुःmanuḥ समिद्धाग्निर्मनसासप्तsamiddhāghnirmanasāsapta होत्र्भिःhotṛbhiḥ |

तta आदित्याādityā अभयंabhayaṃ शर्मśarma यछतyachata सुगाsughā नःकर्तnaḥkarta सुपथाsupathā सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
Ye to whom Manu, by seven priests, with kindled fire, offered the first oblation with his heart and soul,
Vouchsafe us, ye Ādityas, sheitcr free from fear, and make us good and easy paths to happiness.
10.63.7
यya ईशिरेīśire भुवनस्यbhuvanasya परचेतसोpracetaso विश्वस्यviśvasya सथातुर्जगतश्चमन्तवःsthāturjaghataścamantavaḥ |

तेte नःnaḥ कर्तादक्र्तादेनससkṛtādakṛtādenasas पर्यद्याparyadyā देवासःपिप्र्ताdevāsaḥpipṛtā सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
Wise Deities, who have dominion o’er the world, ye thinkers over all that moves not and that moves,
Save us from uncommitted and committed sin, preserve us from all sin to-day for happiness.
10.63.8
भरेष्विन्द्रंbhareṣvindraṃ सुहवंsuhavaṃ हवामहे.अंहोमुचंhavāmahe.aṃhomucaṃ सुक्र्तन्दैव्यंsukṛtandaivyaṃ जनमjanam |

अग्निंaghniṃ मित्रंmitraṃ वरुणंvaruṇaṃ सातयेsātaye भगन्द्यावाप्र्थिवीbhaghandyāvāpṛthivī मरुतःmarutaḥ सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
In battles we invoke Indra still swift to hear, and all the holy Host of Heaven who banish grief,
Agni, Mitra, and Varuṇa that we may gain, Dyays, Bhaga, Maruts, Prthivi for happiness:
10.63.9
सुत्रामाणंsutrāmāṇaṃ पर्थिवींpṛthivīṃ दयामनेहसंdyāmanehasaṃ सुशर्माणमदितिंसुप्रणीतिमsuśarmāṇamaditiṃsupraṇītim |

दैवींdaivīṃ नावंnāvaṃ सवरित्रामनागसमस्रवन्तीमाsvaritrāmanāghasamasravantīmā रुहेमाruhemā सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
Mightily-saving Earth, incomparable Heaven the good guide Aditi who gives secure defence
The well-oared heavenly Ship that lets no waters in, free from defect, will we ascend for happiness.
10.63.10
विश्वेviśve यजत्राyajatrā अधिadhi वोचतोतयेvocatotaye तरायध्वंtrāyadhvaṃ नोno दुरेवायाभिह्रुतःdurevāyāabhihrutaḥ |

सत्ययाsatyayā वोvo देवहूत्याdevahūtyā हुवेमhuvema शर्ण्वतोśṛṇvato देवावसेdevāavase सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
Bless us, all Holy Ones, that we may have your help, guard and protect us from malignant injury.
With fruitful invocation may we call on you, Gods, who give ear to us for grace, for happiness.
10.63.11
अपामीवामपapāmīvāmapa विश्वामनाहुतिमपारातिंviśvāmanāhutimapārātiṃ दुर्विदत्रामघायतःdurvidatrāmaghāyataḥ |

आरेāre देवाdevā दवेषोdveṣo अस्मदasmad युयोतनोरुyuyotanoru णःṇaḥ शर्मयछताśarmayachatā सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
Keep all disease afar and sordid sacrifice, keep off the wicked man's malicious enmity.
Keep far away from us all hatred, O ye Gods, and give us ample shelter for our happiness.
10.63.12
अरिष्टःariṣṭaḥ सsa मर्तोmarto विश्वviśva एधतेedhate परpra परजाभिर्जायतेधर्मणसprajābhirjāyatedharmaṇas परिpari |

यमादित्यासोyamādityāso नयथाnayathā सुनीतिभिरतिविश्वानिsunītibhirativiśvāni दुरिताduritā सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
Untouched by any evil, every mortal thrives, and, following the Law, spreads in his progeny.
Whom ye with your good guidance, O Ādityas, lead safely through all his pain and grief to happiness.
10.63.13
यंyaṃ देवासो.अवथdevāso.avatha वाजसातौvājasātau यंyaṃ शूरसाताśūrasātā मरुतोmaruto हितेधनेhitedhane |

परातर्यावाणंprātaryāvāṇaṃ रथमिन्द्रrathamindra सानसिमरिष्यन्तमारुहेमाsānasimariṣyantamāruhemā सवस्तयेsvastaye ||
That which ye guard and grace in battle, O ye Gods, ye Maruts, where the prize is wealth, where heroes win,
That conquering Car, O Indra, that sets forth at dawn, that never breaks, may we ascend for happiness.
10.63.14
सवस्तिsvasti नःnaḥ पथ्यासुpathyāsu धन्वसुdhanvasu सवस्त्यप्सुsvastyapsu वर्जनेvṛjane सवर्वतिsvarvati |

सवस्तिsvasti नःnaḥ पुत्रक्र्थेषुputrakṛtheṣu योनिषुyoniṣu सवस्तिsvasti रायेrāye मरुतोmaruto दधातनdadhātana ||
Vouchsafe us blessing in our paths and desert tracts, blessing in waters and in battle, for the light;
Blessing upon the wombs that bring male children forth, and blessing, O ye Maruts, for the gain of wealth.
10.63.15
सवस्तिरिदsvastirid धिdhi परपथेprapathe शरेष्ठाśreṣṭhā रेक्णस्वत्यभिrekṇasvatyabhi यावाममेतिyāvāmameti |

साsā नोno अमाamā सोso अरणेaraṇe निni पातुpātu सवावेशाsvāveśā भवतुदेवगोपाbhavatudevaghopā ||
The noblest Svasti with abundant riches, who comes to what is good by distant pathway,—
May she at home and far away preserve us, and dwell with us under the Gods’ protection
10.63.16
एवाevā पलतेःplateḥ सूनुरवीव्र्धदsūnuravīvṛdhad वोvo विश्वviśva आदित्याādityā अदितेमनीषीaditemanīṣī |

ईशानासोīśānāso नरोnaro अमर्त्येनास्ताविamartyenāstāvi जनोjano दिव्योगयेनdivyoghayena ||
Thus hatb the thoughtful sage, the son of Plati, praised you, O Aditi and all Ādityas,
Men are made rich by those who are Immortal: the Heavenly Folk have been extolled by Gaya.
10.63.17
