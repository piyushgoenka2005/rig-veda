HYMN XCVIII. THE GODS.

बर्हस्पतेbṛhaspate परतिprati मेme देवतामिहिdevatāmihi मित्रोmitro वाvā यदyad वरुणोvaruṇo वासिपूषाvāsipūṣā |

आदित्यैर्वाādityairvā यदyad वसुभिर्मरुत्वानvasubhirmarutvān सsa पर्जन्यंशन्तनवेparjanyaṃśantanave वर्षायvṛṣāya ||
COME, be thou Mitra, Varuṇa, or Pūṣan, come, O Bṛhaspati, to mine oblation:
With Maruts, Vasus, or Ādityas, make thou Parjanya pour for Santanu his rain-drops.
10.98.1
आā देवोdevo दूतोdūto अजिरश्चिकित्वानajiraścikitvān तवदtvad देवापेdevāpe अभिabhi मामगछतmāmaghachat |

परतीचीनःpratīcīnaḥ परतिprati मामाmāmā वव्र्त्स्वvavṛtsva दधामिdadhāmi तेte दयुमतींवाचमासनdyumatīṃvācamāsan ||
The God, intelligent, the speedy envoy whom thou hast sent hath come to me, Devapi:
Address thyself to me and turn thee hither within thy lips will I put brilliant language.
10.98.2
अस्मेasme धेहिdhehi दयुमतींdyumatīṃ वाचमासनvācamāsan बर्हस्पतेbṛhaspate अनमीवामिषिरामanamīvāmiṣirām |

ययाyayā वर्ष्टिंvṛṣṭiṃ शन्तनवेśantanave वनावvanāva दिवोdivo दरप्सोमधुमानाdrapsomadhumānā विवेशviveśa ||
Within my mouth, Bṛhaspati, deposit speech lucid, vigorous, and free from weakness,
Thereby to win for Santanu the rain-fall. The meath-rich drop from heaven hath passed within it.
10.98.3
आā नोno दरप्साdrapsā मधुमन्तोmadhumanto विशन्त्विन्द्रviśantvindra देह्यधिरथंसहस्रमdehyadhirathaṃsahasram |

निni षीदṣīda होत्रंhotraṃ रतुथाṛtuthā यजस्वyajasva देवानdevān देवापेहविषाdevāpehaviṣā सपर्यsaparya ||
Let the sweet drops descend on us, O Indra: give us enough to lade a thousand wagons.
Sit to thy Hotar task; pay worship duly, and serve the Gods, Devapi, with oblation.
10.98.4
आर्ष्टिषेणोārṣṭiṣeṇo होत्रंhotraṃ रषिर्निषीदनṛṣirniṣīdan देवापिर्देवसुमतिंचिकित्वानdevāpirdevasumatiṃcikitvān |

सsa उत्तरस्मादधरंuttarasmādadharaṃ समुद्रमपोsamudramapo दिव्याdivyā अस्र्जद्वर्ष्याasṛjadvarṣyā अभिabhi ||
Knowing the God's good-will, Devapi, Ṛṣi, the son of Rstisena, sate as Hotar.
He hath brought down from heaven's most lofty summit the ocean of the rain, celestial waters.
10.98.5
अस्मिनasmin समुद्रेsamudre अध्युत्तरस्मिन्नापोadhyuttarasminnāpo देवेभिर्निव्र्ताdevebhirnivṛtā अतिष्ठनatiṣṭhan |

ताtā अद्रवन्नार्ष्टिषेणेनadravannārṣṭiṣeṇena सर्ष्टाsṛṣṭā देवापिनाdevāpinā परेषिताम्र्क्षिणीषुpreṣitāmṛkṣiṇīṣu ||
Gathered together in that highest ocean, the waters stood by deities obstructed.
They burried down set free by Arstisena, in gaping clefts, urged onward by Devapi.
10.98.6
यदyad देवापिःdevāpiḥ शन्तनवेśantanave पुरोहितोpurohito होत्रायhotrāya वर्तःvṛtaḥ कर्पयन्नदीधेतkṛpayannadīdhet |

देवश्रुतंdevaśrutaṃ वर्ष्टिवनिंvṛṣṭivaniṃ रराणोrarāṇo बर्हस्पतिर्वाचमस्माbṛhaspatirvācamasmā अयछतayachat ||
When as chief priest for Santanu, Devapi, chosen for Hotar's duty, prayed beseeching,
Graciously pleased Bṛhaspati vouchsafed him a voice that reached the Gods and won the waters.
10.98.7
यंyaṃ तवाtvā देवापिःdevāpiḥ शुशुचानोśuśucāno अग्नaghna आर्ष्टिषेणोārṣṭiṣeṇo मनुष्यःसमीधेmanuṣyaḥsamīdhe |

विश्वेभिर्देवैरनुमद्यमानःviśvebhirdevairanumadyamānaḥ परpra पर्जन्यमीरयाparjanyamīrayā वर्ष्टिमन्तमvṛṣṭimantam ||
O Agni whom Devapi Arstisena, the mortal man, hath kindled in his glory,
Joying in him with all the Gods together, urge on the sender of the rain, Parjanya.
10.98.8
तवांtvāṃ पूर्वpūrva रषयोṛṣayo गीर्भिरायनghīrbhirāyan तवामध्वरेषुtvāmadhvareṣu पुरुहूतविश्वेpuruhūtaviśve |

सहस्राण्यधिरथान्यस्मेsahasrāṇyadhirathānyasme आā नोno यज्ञंरोहिदश्वोपyajñaṃrohidaśvopa याहिyāhi ||
All ancient Ṛṣis with their songs approached thee, even thee, O Much-invoked, at sacrifices.
We have provided wagon-loads in thousands: come to the solemn rite, Lord of Red Horses.
10.98.9
एतान्यग्नेetānyaghne नवतिर्नवnavatirnava तवेtve आहुतान्यधिरथाāhutānyadhirathā सहस्रsahasra |

तेभिर्वर्धस्वtebhirvardhasva तन्वःtanvaḥ शूरśūra पूर्वीर्दिवोpūrvīrdivo नोno वर्ष्टिमिषितोरिरीहिvṛṣṭimiṣitorirīhi ||
The wagon-loads, the nine-and-ninety thousand, these have been offered up to thee, O Agni.
Hero, with these increase thy many bodies, and, stimulated, send us rain from heaven.
10.98.10
एतान्यग्नेetānyaghne नवतिंnavatiṃ सहस्राsahasrā संsaṃ परpra यछyacha वर्ष्णvṛṣṇa इन्द्रायभागमindrāyabhāgham |

विद्वानvidvān पथpatha रतुशोṛtuśo देवयानानप्यौलानंdevayānānapyaulānaṃ दिविदेवेषुdivideveṣu धेहिdhehi ||
Give thou these ninety thousand loads, O Agni, to Indra, to the Bull, to be his portion.
Knowing the paths which Deities duly travel, set mid the Gods in heaven Aulana also.
10.98.11
अग्नेaghne बाधस्वbādhasva विvi मर्धोmṛdho विvi दुर्गहापामीवामपरक्षांसिdurghahāpāmīvāmaparakṣāṃsi सेधsedha |

अस्मातasmāt समुद्रादsamudrād बर्हतोbṛhato दिवोdivo नो.अपाम्भूमानमुपno.apāmbhūmānamupa नःnaḥ सर्जेहsṛjeha ||
O Agni, drive afar our foes, our troubles chase malady away and wicked demons.
From this air-ocean, from the lofty heavens, send down on us a mighty flood of waters.
10.98.12
