HYMN XCII. VIŚVEDEVAS.

यज्ञस्यyajñasya वोvo रथ्यंrathyaṃ विश्पतिंviśpatiṃ विशांviśāṃ होतारमक्तोरतिथिंhotāramaktoratithiṃ विभावसुमvibhāvasum |

शोचञ्छुष्कासुśocañchuṣkāsu हरिणीषुhariṇīṣu जर्भुरद्व्र्षाjarbhuradvṛṣā केतुर्यजतोketuryajato दयामशायतdyāmaśāyata ||
I PRAISE your Charioteer of sacrifice, the Lord of men, Priest of the tribes, refulgent, Guest of night.
Blazing amid dry plants, snatching amid the green, the Strong, the Holy Herald hath attained to heaven.
10.92.1
इममञ्जस्पामुभयेimamañjaspāmubhaye अक्र्ण्वतakṛṇvata धर्माणमग्निंdharmāṇamaghniṃ विदथस्यसाधनमvidathasyasādhanam |

अक्तुंaktuṃ नna यह्वमुषसःyahvamuṣasaḥ पुरोहितंpurohitaṃ तनूनपातमरुषस्यtanūnapātamaruṣasya निंसतेniṃsate ||
Him, Agni, Gods and men have made their chief support, who drinks the fatness and completes the sacrifice.
With kisses they caress the Grandson of the Red, like the swift ray of light, the Household Priest of Dawn.
10.92.2
बळbaḷ अस्यasya नीथाnīthā विvi पणेश्चpaṇeśca मन्महेmanmahe वयाvayā अस्यasya परहुताासुरत्तवेprahutāāsurattave |

यदाyadā घोरासोghorāso अम्र्तत्वमाशतादिज्जनस्यदैव्यस्यamṛtatvamāśatādijjanasyadaivyasya चर्किरनcarkiran ||
Yea, we discriminate his and the niggard's ways: his branches evermore are sent forth to consume.
When his terrific flames have reached the Immortal's world, then men remember and extol the Heavenly Folk.
10.92.3
रतस्यṛtasya हिhi परसितिर्द्यौरुरुprasitirdyaururu वयचोvyaco नमोnamo मह्यरमतिःपनीयसीmahyaramatiḥpanīyasī |

इन्द्रोindro मित्रोmitro वरुणःvaruṇaḥ संsaṃ चिकित्रिरे.अथोcikitrire.atho भगःसविताbhaghaḥsavitā पूतदक्षसःpūtadakṣasaḥ ||
For then the net of Law, Dyaus, and the wide expanse, Earth, Worship, and Devotion meet for highest praise,
Varuṇa, Indra, Mitra were of one accord, and Savitar and Bhaga, Lords of holy might.
10.92.4
परpra रुद्रेणrudreṇa ययिनाyayinā यन्तिyanti सिन्धवस्तिरोsindhavastiro महीमरमतिन्दधन्विरेmahīmaramatindadhanvire |

येभिःyebhiḥ परिज्माparijmā परियन्नुरुpariyannuru जरयोjrayo विvi रोरुवज्जठरेroruvajjaṭhare विश्वमुक्षतेviśvamukṣate ||
Onward, with ever-roaming Rudra, speed the floods: over Aramati the Mighty have they run.
With them Parijman, moving round his vast domain, loud bellowing, bedews all things that are within.
10.92.5
कराणाkrāṇā रुद्राrudrā मरुतोmaruto विश्वक्र्ष्टयोviśvakṛṣṭayo दिवःdivaḥ शयेनासोसुरस्यśyenāsoasurasya नीळयःnīḷayaḥ |

तेभिश्चष्टेtebhiścaṣṭe वरुणोvaruṇo मित्रोmitro अर्यमेन्द्रोदेवेभिरर्वशेभिरर्वशःaryamendrodevebhirarvaśebhirarvaśaḥ ||
Straightway the Rudras, Maruts visiting all men, Falcons of Dyaus, home-dwellers with the Asura,—
Varuṇa, Mitra, Aryaman look on with these, and the swift-moving Indra with swift-moving Gods.
10.92.6
इन्द्रेindre भुजंbhujaṃ शशमानासśaśamānāsa आशतāśata सूरोsūro दर्शीकेdṛśīke वर्षणश्चvṛṣaṇaśca पौंस्येpauṃsye |

परpra येye नवस्यार्हणाnvasyārhaṇā ततक्षिरेtatakṣire युजंyujaṃ वज्रंन्र्षदनेषुvajraṃnṛṣadaneṣu कारवःkāravaḥ ||
With Indra have they found enjoyment, they who toil, in the light's beauty, in the very Strong One's strength;
The singers who in men's assemblies forged for him, according to his due, his friend the thunderbolt.
10.92.7
सूरश्चिदाsūraścidā हरितोharito अस्यasya रीरमदिन्द्रादाrīramadindrādā कश्चिदkaścid भयतेतवीयसःbhayatetavīyasaḥ |

भीमस्यbhīmasya वर्ष्णोvṛṣṇo जठरादभिश्वसोjaṭharādabhiśvaso दिवे-दिवेसहुरिdive-divesahuri सतन्नबाधितःstannabādhitaḥ ||
Even the Sun's Bay Coursers hath lie held in check: each one fears Indra as the mightiest of all.
Unhindered, from the air's vault thunders day by day the loud triumphant breathing of the fearful Bull.
10.92.8
सतोमंstomaṃ वोvo अद्यadya रुद्रायrudrāya शिक्वसेśikvase कषयद्वीरायkṣayadvīrāya नमसादिदिष्टनnamasādidiṣṭana |

येभिःyebhiḥ शिवःśivaḥ सववानेवयावभिर्दिवःसिषक्तिsvavānevayāvabhirdivaḥsiṣakti सवयशाsvayaśā निकामभिःnikāmabhiḥ ||
With humble adoration show this day your song of praise to mighty Rudra, Ruler of the brave:
With whom, the Eager Ones, going their ordered course, he comes from heaven Self-bright, auspicious, strong to guard.
10.92.9
तेte हिhi परजायाprajāyā अभरन्तabharanta विvi शरवोśravo बर्हस्पतिर्व्र्षभःसोमजामयःbṛhaspatirvṛṣabhaḥsomajāmayaḥ |

यज्ञैरथर्वाyajñairatharvā परथमोprathamo विvi धारयदdhārayad देवादक्षैर्भ्र्गवःdevādakṣairbhṛghavaḥ संsaṃ चिकित्रिरेcikitrire ||
For these have spread abroad the fame of human kind, the Bull Bṛhaspati and Soma's brotherhood.
Atharvan first by sacrifices made men sure: through skill the Bhṛgus were esteemed of all as Gods.
10.92.10
तेte हिhi दयावाप्र्थिवीdyāvāpṛthivī भूरिरेतसाbhūriretasā नराशंसश्चतुरङगोयमो.अदितिःnarāśaṃsaścaturaṅghoyamo.aditiḥ |

देवस्त्वष्टाdevastvaṣṭā दरविणोदाdraviṇodā रभुक्षणःṛbhukṣaṇaḥ पररोदसीprarodasī मरुतोmaruto विष्णुरर्हिरेviṣṇurarhire ||
For these, the Earth and Heaven with their abundant seed, four-bodied Narāśaṁsa, Yama, Aditi,
God Tvaṣṭar Wealth-bestower, the Ṛbhukṣaṇas, Rodasī, Maruts, Viṣṇu, claim and merit praise.
10.92.11
उतuta सयsya नna उशिजामुर्वियाuśijāmurviyā कविरहिःkavirahiḥ शर्णोतुśṛṇotu बुध्न्योहवीमनिbudhnyohavīmani |

सूर्यामासाsūryāmāsā विचरन्ताvicarantā दिविक्षिताdivikṣitā धियाशमीनहुषीdhiyāśamīnahuṣī अस्यasya बोधतमbodhatam ||
And may he too give car, the Sage, from far away, the Dragon of the Deep, to this our yearning call.
Ye Sun and Moon who dwell in heaven and move in turn, and with your thought, O Earth and Sky, observe this well.
10.92.12
परpra नःnaḥ पूषाpūṣā चरथंcarathaṃ विश्वदेव्यो.अपांviśvadevyo.apāṃ नपादवतुवायुरिष्टयेnapādavatuvāyuriṣṭaye |

आत्मानंātmānaṃ वस्योvasyo अभिabhi वातमर्चतvātamarcata तदश्विनाtadaśvinā सुहवाsuhavā यामनिyāmani शरुतमśrutam ||
Dear to all Gods, may Pūṣan guard the ways we go, the Waters’ child and Vāyu help us to success.
Sing lauds for your great bliss to Wind, the breath of all: ye Aśvins prompt to hear, hear this upon your way.
10.92.13
विशामासामभयानामधिक्षितंviśāmāsāmabhayānāmadhikṣitaṃ गीर्भिरुghīrbhiru सवयशसंग्र्णीमसिsvayaśasaṃghṛṇīmasi |

गनाभिर्विश्वाभिरदितिमनर्वणमक्तोर्युवानंghnābhirviśvābhiraditimanarvaṇamaktoryuvānaṃ नर्मणाnṛmaṇā अधाadhā पतिमpatim ||
With hymns of praise we sing him who is throned as Lord over these fearless tribes, the Self-resplendent One.
We praise Night's youthful Lord benevolent to men, the foeless One, the free, with all celestial Dames.
10.92.14
रेभदत्रrebhadatra जनुषाjanuṣā पूर्वोpūrvo अङगिराaṅghirā गरावाणghrāvāṇa ऊर्ध्वाūrdhvā अभिचक्षुरध्वरमabhicakṣuradhvaram |

येभिर्विहायाyebhirvihāyā अभवदabhavad विचक्षणःपाथःvicakṣaṇaḥpāthaḥ सुमेकंsumekaṃ सवधितिर्वनन्वतिsvadhitirvananvati ||
By reason of his birth here Aṅgiras first sang: the pressing-stones upraised beheld the sacrifice-
The stones through which the Sage became exceeding vast, and the sharp axe obtains in fight the beauteous place.
10.92.15
