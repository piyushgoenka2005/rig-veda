HYMN CIV. INDRA.

असाविasāvi सोमःsomaḥ पुरुहूतpuruhūta तुभ्यंtubhyaṃ हरिभ्यांharibhyāṃ यज्ञमुपyajñamupa याहितूयमyāhitūyam |

तुभ्यंtubhyaṃ गिरोghiro विप्रवीराvipravīrā इयानाiyānā दधन्विरdadhanvira इन्द्रपिबाindrapibā सुतस्यsutasya ||
Soma hath flowed for thee, Invoked of mat Speed to our sacrifice with both thy Coursers.
To thee have streameld the songs or mighty singers, imploring, Indra, drink of our libation.
10.104.1
अप्सुapsu धूतस्यdhūtasya हरिवःharivaḥ पिबेहpibeha नर्भिःnṛbhiḥ सुतस्यsutasya जठरम्प्र्णस्वjaṭharampṛṇasva |

मिमिक्षुर्यमद्रयmimikṣuryamadraya इन्द्रindra तुभ्यंtubhyaṃ तेभिर्वर्धस्वमदमुक्थवाहःtebhirvardhasvamadamukthavāhaḥ ||
Drink of the juice which men have washed in waters, and fill thee full, O Lord of Tawny Horses.
O Indra, hearer of the laud, with Soma which stones have mixed for thee enhance thy rapture.
10.104.2
परोग्रांproghrāṃ पीतिंpītiṃ वर्ष्णvṛṣṇa इयर्मिiyarmi सत्यांsatyāṃ परयैprayai सुतस्यहर्यश्वsutasyaharyaśva तुभ्यमtubhyam |

इन्द्रindra धेनाभिरिहdhenābhiriha मादयस्वmādayasva धीभिर्विश्वाभिःdhībhirviśvābhiḥ शच्याśacyā गर्णानःghṛṇānaḥ ||
To make thee start, a strong true draught I offer to thee, the Bull, O thou whom Bay Steeds carry.
Here take delight, O Indra, in our voices while thou art hymned with power and all our spirit.
10.104.3
ऊतीūtī शचीवस्तवśacīvastava वीर्येणvīryeṇa वयोvayo दधानाdadhānā उशिजuśija रतज्ञाःṛtajñāḥ |

परजावदिन्द्रprajāvadindra मनुषोmanuṣo दुरोणेduroṇe तस्थुर्ग्र्णन्तःसधमाद्यासःtasthurghṛṇantaḥsadhamādyāsaḥ ||
O Mighty Indra, through thine aid, thy prowess, obtaining life, zealous, and skilled in Order,
Men in the house who share the sacred banquet stand singing praise that brings them store of children.
10.104.4
परणीतिभिषpraṇītibhiṣ टेṭe हर्यश्वharyaśva सुष्टोःsuṣṭoḥ सुषुम्नस्यsuṣumnasya पुरुरुचोजनासःpururucojanāsaḥ |

मंहिष्ठामूतिंmaṃhiṣṭhāmūtiṃ वितिरेvitire दधानाdadhānā सतोतारैन्द्रstotāraindra तवtava सून्र्ताभिःsūnṛtābhiḥ ||
Through thy directions, Lord of Tawny Coursers, thine who art firm, splendid, and blest, the people
Obtain most liberal aid for their salvation, and praise thee, Indra, through thine excellencies.
10.104.5
उपupa बरह्माणिbrahmāṇi हरिवोharivo हरिभ्यांharibhyāṃ सोमस्यsomasya याहिyāhi पीतयेpītaye सुतस्यsutasya |

इन्द्रindra तवाtvā यज्ञःyajñaḥ कषममाणमानडkṣamamāṇamānaḍ दाश्वानस्यध्वरस्यdāśvānasyadhvarasya परकेतःpraketaḥ ||
Lord of the Bays, come with thy two Bay Horses, come to our prayers, to drink the juice of Soma.
To thee comes sacrifice which thou acceptest: thou, skilled in holy rites, art he who giveth.
10.104.6
सहस्रवाजमभिमातिषाहंsahasravājamabhimātiṣāhaṃ सुतेरणंsuteraṇaṃ मघवानंmaghavānaṃ सुव्र्क्तिमsuvṛktim |

उपupa भूषन्तिbhūṣanti गिरोghiro अप्रतीतमिन्द्रंapratītamindraṃ नमस्याnamasyā जरितुःपनन्तjarituḥpananta ||
Him of a thousand powers, subduing foemen, Maghavan praised with hymns and pleased with Soma,—
Even him our songs approach, resistless Indra: the adorations of the singer laud him.
10.104.7
सप्तापोsaptāpo देवीःdevīḥ सुरणाsuraṇā अम्र्क्ताamṛktā याभिःyābhiḥ सिन्धुमतरsindhumatara इन्द्रपूर्भितindrapūrbhit |

नवतिंnavatiṃ सरोत्याsrotyā नवnava चca सरवन्तीर्देवेभ्योsravantīrdevebhyo गातुम्मनुषेghātummanuṣe चca विन्दःvindaḥ ||
The way to bliss for Gods and man thou foundest, Indra, seven lovely floods, divine, untroubled,
Wherewith thou, rending forts, didst move the ocean, and nine-and-ninety flowing streams of water.
10.104.8
अपोapo महीरभिशस्तेरमुञ्चो.अजागरास्वधिmahīrabhiśasteramuñco.ajāgharāsvadhi देवdeva एकःekaḥ |

इन्द्रindra यास्त्वंyāstvaṃ वर्त्रतूर्येvṛtratūrye चकर्थcakartha ताभिर्विश्वायुस्तन्वंtābhirviśvāyustanvaṃ पुपुष्याःpupuṣyāḥ ||
Thou from the curse didst free the mighty Waters, and as their only God didst watch and guard them.
O Indra, cherish evermore thy body with those which thou hast won in quelling Vṛtra.
10.104.9
वीरेण्यःvīreṇyaḥ करतुरिन्द्रःkraturindraḥ सुशस्तिरुतापिsuśastirutāpi धेनाdhenā पुरुहूतमीट्टेpuruhūtamīṭṭe |

आर्दयदārdayad वर्त्रमक्र्णोदुvṛtramakṛṇodu लोकंlokaṃ ससाहेsasāhe शक्रःप्र्तनाśakraḥpṛtanā अभिष्टिःabhiṣṭiḥ ||
Heroic power and noble praise is Indra yea, the song worships him invoked of many.
Vṛtra he quelled, and gave men room and freedom: gakra, victorious, hath conquered armies.
10.104.10
शुनंśunaṃ हुवेमhuvema मघवानमिन्द्रंmaghavānamindraṃ ...... ||
Call we on Maghayan, auspicious Indra. best Hero in this fight where spoil is gathered,
The Strong, who listens, who gives aid in battles, who slays the Vṛtras, wins and gathers riches.
10.104.11
