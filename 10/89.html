HYMN LXXXIX. INDRA.

इन्द्रंindraṃ सतवाstavā नर्तमंnṛtamaṃ यस्यyasya मह्नाmahnā विबबाधेvibabādhe रोचनाrocanā विvi जमोन्तानjmoantān |

आā यःyaḥ पप्रौpaprau चर्षणीध्र्दcarṣaṇīdhṛd वरोभिःvarobhiḥ परसिन्धुभ्योprasindhubhyo रिरिचानोriricāno महित्वाmahitvā ||
I WILL extol the most heroic Indra who with his might forced earth and sky asunder;
Who hath filled all with width as man's Upholder, surpassing floods and rivers in his greatness.
10.89.1
सsa सूर्यःsūryaḥ पर्युरूparyurū वरांस्येन्द्रोvarāṃsyendro वव्र्त्यादvavṛtyād रथ्येवचक्राrathyevacakrā |

अतिष्ठन्तमपस्यंatiṣṭhantamapasyaṃ नna सर्गंsarghaṃ कर्ष्णाkṛṣṇā तमांसित्विष्याtamāṃsitviṣyā जघानjaghāna ||
Sūrya is he: throughout the wide expanses shall Indra turn him, swift as car-wheels, hither,
Like a stream resting not but ever active he hath destroyed, with light, the black-hued darkness.
10.89.2
समानमस्माsamānamasmā अनपाव्र्दर्चanapāvṛdarca कष्मयाkṣmayā दिवोdivo असमंasamaṃ बरह्मनव्यमbrahmanavyam |

विvi यःyaḥ पर्ष्ठेवpṛṣṭheva जनिमान्यर्यjanimānyarya इन्द्रश्चिकायindraścikāya नसखायमीषेnasakhāyamīṣe ||
To him I sing a holy prayer, incessant new, matchless, common to the earth and heaven,
Who marks, as they were backs, all living creatures: ne’er doth he fail a friend, the noble Indra.
10.89.3
इन्द्रायindrāya गिरोghiro अनिशितसर्गाaniśitasarghā अपःapaḥ परेरयंprerayaṃ सगरस्यsagharasya बुध्नातbudhnāt |

योyo अक्षेणेवakṣeṇeva चक्रियाcakriyā शचीभिर्विष्वकśacībhirviṣvak तस्तम्भप्र्थिवीमुतtastambhapṛthivīmuta दयामdyām ||
I will send forth my songs in flow unceasing, like water from the ocean's depth, to Indra.
Who to his car on both its sides securely hath fixed the earth and heaven as with an axle.
10.89.4
आपान्तमन्युस्त्र्पलप्रभर्माāpāntamanyustṛpalaprabharmā धुनिःdhuniḥ शिमीवाञ्छरुमान्र्जीषीśimīvāñcharumānṛjīṣī |

सोमोsomo विश्वान्यतसाviśvānyatasā वनानिvanāni नार्वागिन्द्रम्प्रतिमानानिnārvāghindrampratimānāni देभुःdebhuḥ ||
Rousing with draughts, the Shaker, rushing onward, impetuous, very strong, armed as with arrows
Is Soma; forest trees and all the bushes deceive not Indra with their offered likeness.
10.89.5
नna यस्यyasya दयावाप्र्थिवीdyāvāpṛthivī नna धन्वdhanva नान्तरिक्षंnāntarikṣaṃ नाद्रयःसोमोnādrayaḥsomo अक्षाःakṣāḥ |

यदस्यyadasya मन्युरधिनीयमानःmanyuradhinīyamānaḥ सर्णातिsṛṇāti वीळुरुजतिvīḷurujati सथिराणिsthirāṇi ||
Soma hath flowed to him whom naught can equal, the earth, the heavens, the firmament, the mountains,—
When heightened in his ire his indignation shatters the firm and breaks the strong in pieces.
10.89.6
जघानjaghāna वर्त्रंvṛtraṃ सवधितिर्वनेवsvadhitirvaneva रुरोजruroja पुरोpuro अरदनaradan नसिन्धूनnasindhūn |

बिभेदbibheda गिरिंghiriṃ नवमिनnavamin नna कुम्भमाkumbhamā गाghā इन्द्रोक्र्णुतindroakṛṇuta सवयुग्भिःsvayughbhiḥ ||
As an axe fells the tree so be slew Vṛtra, brake down the strongholds and dug out the rivers.
He cleft the mountain like a new-made pitcher. Indra brought forth the kine with his Companions.
10.89.7
तवंtvaṃ हha तयदtyad रणयाṛṇayā इन्द्रindra धीरो.असिर्नdhīro.asirna पर्वparva वर्जिनाश्र्णासिvṛjināśṛṇāsi |

परpra येye मित्रस्यmitrasya वरुणस्यvaruṇasya धामdhāma युजंyujaṃ नna जनामिनन्तिjanāminanti मित्रमmitram ||
Wise art thou, Punisher of guilt, O Indra. The sword lops limbs, thou smitest down the sinner,
The men who injure, as it were a comrade, the lofty Law of Varuṇa and Mitra.
10.89.8
परpra येye मित्रंmitraṃ परार्यमणंprāryamaṇaṃ दुरेवाःdurevāḥ परpra संगिरःsaṃghiraḥ परवरुणंpravaruṇaṃ मिनन्तिminanti |

नयमित्रेषुnyamitreṣu वधमिन्द्रvadhamindra तुम्रंtumraṃ वर्षन्व्र्षाणमरुषंvṛṣanvṛṣāṇamaruṣaṃ शिशीहिśiśīhi ||
Men who lead evil lives, who break agreements, and injure Varuṇa, Aryaman and Mitra,—
Against these foes, O Mighty Indra, sharpen, as furious death, thy Bull of fiery colour.
10.89.9
इन्द्रोindro दिवdiva इन्द्रindra ईशेīśe पर्थिव्याpṛthivyā इन्द्रोindro अपामिन्द्रapāmindra इत्पर्वतानामitparvatānām |

इन्द्रोindro वर्धामिन्द्रvṛdhāmindra इनin मेधिराणामिन्द्रःक्षेमेmedhirāṇāmindraḥkṣeme योगेyoghe हव्यhavya इन्द्रःindraḥ ||
Indra is Sovran Lord of Earth and Heaven, Indra is Lord of waters and of mountains.
Indra is Lord of prosperers and sages Indra must be invoked in rest and effort.
10.89.10
पराक्तुभ्यprāktubhya इन्द्रःindraḥ परpra वर्धोvṛdho अहभ्यःahabhyaḥ परान्तरिक्षातprāntarikṣāt परसमुद्रस्यprasamudrasya धासेःdhāseḥ |

परpra वातस्यvātasya परथसःprathasaḥ परpra जमोjmo अन्तात्प्रantātpra सिन्धुभ्योsindhubhyo रिरिचेririce परpra कषितिभ्यःkṣitibhyaḥ ||
Vaster than days and nights, Giver of increase, vaster than firmament and flood of ocean,
Vaster than bounds of earth and wind's extension, vaster than rivers and our lands is Indra.
10.89.11
परpra शोशुचत्याśośucatyā उषसोuṣaso नna केतुरसिन्वाketurasinvā तेte वर्ततामिन्द्रहेतिःvartatāmindrahetiḥ |

अश्मेवaśmeva विध्यvidhya दिवdiva आā सर्जानस्तपिष्ठेनsṛjānastapiṣṭhena हेषसाद्रोघमित्रानheṣasādroghamitrān ||
Forward, as herald of refulgent Morning, let thine insatiate arrow fly, O Indra.
And pierce, as ’twere a stone launched forth from heaven, with hottest blaze the men who love deception.
10.89.12
अन्वहanvaha मासाmāsā अन्विदanvid वनान्यन्वोषधीरनुvanānyanvoṣadhīranu पर्वतासःparvatāsaḥ |

अन्विन्द्रंanvindraṃ रोदसीrodasī वावशानेvāvaśāne अन्वापोanvāpo अजिहतजायमानमajihatajāyamānam ||
Him, verily, the moons, the mountains followed, the tall trees followed and the plants and herbage.
Yearning with love both Worlds approached, the Waters waited on Indra when he first had being.
10.89.13
कर्हिkarhi सवितsvit साsā तta इन्द्रindra चेत्यासदघस्यcetyāsadaghasya यदyad भिनदोbhinado रक्षेषतrakṣaeṣat |

मित्रक्रुवोmitrakruvo यच्छसनेyacchasane नna गावःghāvaḥ पर्थिव्याpṛthivyā आप्र्गमुयाāpṛghamuyā शयन्तेśayante ||
Where was the vengeful dart when thou, O Indra, clavest the demon ever beat on outrage?
When fiends lay there upon the ground extended like cattle in the place of immolation?
10.89.14
शत्रूयन्तोśatrūyanto अभिabhi येye नस्ततस्रेnastatasre महिmahi वराधन्तvrādhanta ओगणासैन्द्रoghaṇāsaindra |

अन्धेनामित्रास्तमसाandhenāmitrāstamasā सचन्तांsacantāṃ सुज्योतिषोsujyotiṣo अक्तवस्तानभिaktavastānabhi षयुःṣyuḥ ||
Those who are set in enmity against us, the Ogaṇas, O Indra, waxen mighty,—
Let blinding darkness follow those our foemen, while these shall have bright shining nights to light them.
10.89.15
पुरूणिpurūṇi हिhi तवाtvā सवनाsavanā जनानांjanānāṃ बरह्माणिbrahmāṇi मन्दनmandan गर्णताम्र्षीणामghṛṇatāmṛṣīṇām |

इमामाघोषन्नवसाimāmāghoṣannavasā सहूतिंsahūtiṃ तिरोtiro विश्वानर्चतोviśvānarcato याह्यर्वांyāhyarvāṃ ||
May plentiful libations of the people, and singing Ṛṣis’ holy prayers rejoice thee.
Hearing with love this common invocation, come unto us, pass by all those who praise thee.
10.89.16
एवाevā तेte वयमिन्द्रvayamindra भुञ्जतीनांbhuñjatīnāṃ विद्यामvidyāma सुमतीनांनवानामsumatīnāṃnavānām |

विद्यामvidyāma वस्तोरवसाvastoravasā गर्णन्तोghṛṇanto विश्वामित्राviśvāmitrā उततutata इन्द्रindra नूनमnūnam ||
O Indra, thus may we be made partakers of thy new favours that shall bring us profit.
Singing with love, may we the Viśvāmitras win daylight even now through thee, O Indra.
10.89.17
शुनंśunaṃ हुवेमhuvema मघवानंmaghavānaṃ ...... ||
Call we on Maghavan, auspicious Indra, best hero in the fight where spoil is gathered,
The Strong who listens, who gives aid in battles, who slays the Vṛtras, wins and gathers riches.
10.89.18
