HYMN XVII. INDRA.

तदस्मैtadasmai नव्यमङगिरस्वदर्चतnavyamaṅghirasvadarcata शुष्माśuṣmā यदस्यyadasya परत्नथोदीरतेpratnathodīrate |

विश्वाviśvā यदyad गोत्राghotrā सहसाsahasā परीव्र्ताparīvṛtā मदेmade सोमस्यsomasya दरंहितान्यैरयतdṛṃhitānyairayat ||
LIKE the Aṅgirases, sing this new song forth to him, for, as in ancient days, his mighty powers are shown,
When in the rapture of the Soma he unclosed with strength the solid firm-shut stables of the kine.
2.17.1
सsa भुतुbhutu योyo हha परथमायprathamāya धायसdhāyasa ओजोojo मिमानोmimāno महिमानमातिरतmahimānamātirat |

शूरोśūro योyo युत्सुyutsu तन्वंtanvaṃ परिव्यतparivyata शीर्षणिśīrṣaṇi दयांdyāṃ महिनाmahinā परत्यमुञ्चतpratyamuñcata ||
Let him be even that God who, for the earliest draught measuring out his power, increased his majesty;
Hero who fortified his body in the wars, and through his greatness set the heaven upon his head.
2.17.2
अधाक्र्णोःadhākṛṇoḥ परथमंprathamaṃ वीर्यंvīryaṃ महदmahad यदस्याग्रेyadasyāghre बरह्मणाbrahmaṇā शुष्ममैरयःśuṣmamairayaḥ |

रथेष्ठेनratheṣṭhena हर्यश्वेनharyaśvena विच्युताःvicyutāḥ परpra जीरयःjīrayaḥ सिस्रतेsisrate सध्र्यकsadhryak पर्थकpṛthak ||
Thou didst perform thy first great deed of hero might what time thou showedst power, through prayer, before this folk.
Hurled down by thee the car-borne Lord of Tawny Steeds, the congregated swift ones fled in sundry ways.
2.17.3
अधाadhā योyo विश्वाviśvā भुवनाभिbhuvanābhi मज्मनेशानक्र्तmajmaneśānakṛt परवयाpravayā अभ्यवर्धतabhyavardhata |

आदād रोदसीrodasī जयोतिषाjyotiṣā वह्निरातनोतvahnirātanot सीव्यनsīvyan तमांसिtamāṃsi दुधिताdudhitā समव्ययतsamavyayat ||
He made himself by might Lord of all living things, and strong in vital power waxed great above them all.
He, borne on high, o’erspread with light the heaven and earth, and, sewing up the turbid darkness, closed it in.
2.17.4
सsa पराचीनानprācīnān पर्वतानparvatān दरंहदोजसाधराचीनमक्र्णोदपामपःdṛṃhadojasādharācīnamakṛṇodapāmapaḥ |

अधारयतadhārayat पर्थिवींpṛthivīṃ विश्वधायसमस्तभ्नानviśvadhāyasamastabhnān माययाmāyayā दयामवस्रसःdyāmavasrasaḥ ||
He with his might made firm the forward-bending hills, the downward rushing of the waters he ordained.
Fast he upheld the earth that nourisheth all life, and stayed the heaven from falling by his wondrous skill.
2.17.5
सास्माsāsmā अरंaraṃ बाहुभ्यांbāhubhyāṃ यंyaṃ पिताक्र्णोदpitākṛṇod विश्वस्मादाviśvasmādā जनुषोjanuṣo वेदससvedasas परिpari |

येनाyenā पर्थिव्यांpṛthivyāṃ निni करिविंkriviṃ शयध्यैśayadhyai वज्रेणvajreṇa हत्व्यव्र्णकhatvyavṛṇak तुविष्वणिःtuviṣvaṇiḥ ||
Fit for the grasping of his arms is what the Sire hath fabricated from all kind of precious wealth.
The thunderbolt, wherewith, loud-roaring, he smote down, and striking him to death laid Krivi on the earth.
2.17.6
अमाजूरिवamājūriva पित्रोःpitroḥ सचाsacā सतीsatī समानादाsamānādā सदसस्त्वामियेsadasastvāmiye भगमbhagham |

कर्धिkṛdhi परकेतमुपpraketamupa मास्याmāsyā भरbhara दद्धिdaddhi भागंbhāghaṃ तन्वोtanvo येनyena मामहःmāmahaḥ ||
As she who in her parents' house is growing old, I pray to thee as Bhaga from the seat of all.
Grant knowledge, mete it out and bring it to us here: give us the share wherewith thou makest people glad.
2.17.7
भोजंbhojaṃ तवामिन्द्रtvāmindra वयंvayaṃ हुवेमhuvema ददिषdadiṣ टवमिन्द्रापांसिṭvamindrāpāṃsi वाजानvājān |

अविड्ढीन्द्रaviḍḍhīndra चित्रयाcitrayā नna ऊतिūti कर्धिkṛdhi वर्शन्निन्द्रvṛśannindra वस्यसोvasyaso नःnaḥ ||
May we invoke thee as a liberal giver thou givest us, O Indra, strength and labours.
Help us with manifold assistance, Indra:
Mighty
One, Indra, make us yet more wealthy.
2.17.8
नूनंnūnaṃ साsā ...... ||
Now may that wealthy Cow of thine, O Indra, give in return a boon to him who lauds thee.
Give to thy praisers: let not fortune fail us. Loud may we speak, with heroes, in assembly.
2.17.9
