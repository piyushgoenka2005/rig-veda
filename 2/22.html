HYMN XXII. INDRA.

तरिकद्रुकेषुtrikadrukeṣu महिषोmahiṣo यवाशिरंyavāśiraṃ तुविशुष्मस्त्र्पतtuviśuṣmastṛpat सोममपिबदsomamapibad विष्णुनाviṣṇunā सुतंsutaṃ यथावशतyathāvaśat |

सsa ईंīṃ ममादmamāda महिmahi कर्मkarma कर्तवेkartave महामुरुंmahāmuruṃ सैनंsainaṃ सश्चदsaścad देवोdevo देवंdevaṃ सत्यमिन्द्रंसत्यsatyamindraṃsatya इन्दुःinduḥ ||
At the Trikadrukas the Great and Strong hath drunk drink blent with meal. With Viṣṇu hath he quaffed the poured out Soma juice, all that he would.
That hath so heightened him the Great, the Wide, to do his mighty work.
So may the God attain the God, true Indu Indra who is true.
2.22.1
अधadha तविषीमानभ्योजसाtviṣīmānabhyojasā करिविंkriviṃ युधाभवदाyudhābhavadā रोदसीrodasī अप्र्णदस्यapṛṇadasya मज्मनाmajmanā परpra वाव्र्धेvāvṛdhe |

अधत्तान्यंadhattānyaṃ जठरेjaṭhare परेमरिच्यतpremaricyata सैनंsainaṃ ...... ||
So he resplendent in the battle overcame Krivi by might. He with his majesty hath filled the earth and heaven, and waxen strong.
One share of the libation hath he swallowed down: one share he left.
So may the God attend the God, true Indu Indra who is true.
2.22.2
साकंsākaṃ जातःjātaḥ करतुनाkratunā साकमोजसाsākamojasā ववक्षिथvavakṣitha साकंsākaṃ वर्द्धोvṛddho वीर्यैःvīryaiḥ सासहिर्म्र्धोsāsahirmṛdho विचर्षणिःvicarṣaṇiḥ |

दाताdātā राधःrādhaḥ सतुवतेstuvate काम्यंkāmyaṃ वसुvasu सैनंsainaṃ ...... ||
Brought forth together with wisdom and mighty power thou grewest great; with hero deeds subduing the malevolent, most swift in act;
Giving prosperity, and lovely wealth to him who praiseth thee. So may the God attend the God, true Indu Indra who is true.
2.22.3
तवtava तयनtyan नर्यंnaryaṃ नर्तो.अपnṛto.apa इन्द्रindra परथमंprathamaṃ पूर्व्यंpūrvyaṃ दिविdivi परवाच्यंpravācyaṃ कर्तमkṛtam |

यदyad देवस्यdevasya शवसाśavasā परारिणाprāriṇā असुंasuṃ रिणन्नपःriṇannapaḥ |

भुवदbhuvad विश्वमभ्यादेवमोजसाviśvamabhyādevamojasā विदादूर्जंvidādūrjaṃ शतक्रतुर्विदादिषमśatakraturvidādiṣam ||
This, Indra, was thy hero deed, Dancer, thy first and ancient work, worthy to be told forth in heaven,
What time thou sentest down life with a God's own power, freeing the floods.
All that is godless may he conquer with his might, and, Lord of Hundred Powers, find for us strength and food.
