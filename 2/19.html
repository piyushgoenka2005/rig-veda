HYMN XIX. INDRA.

अपाय्यस्यान्धसोapāyyasyāndhaso मदायmadāya मनीषिणःmanīṣiṇaḥ सुवानस्यsuvānasya परयसःprayasaḥ |

यस्मिन्निन्द्रःyasminnindraḥ परदिविpradivi वाव्र्धानvāvṛdhāna ओकोoko दधेdadhe बरह्मण्यन्तश्चbrahmaṇyantaśca नरःnaraḥ ||
DRAUGHTS of this sweet juice have been drunk for rapture, of the wise Soma-presser's offered dainty,
Wherein, grown mighty in the days aforetime, Indra hath found delight, and men who worship.
2.19.1
अस्यasya मन्दानोmandāno मध्वोmadhvo वज्रहस्तो.अहिमिन्द्रोvajrahasto.ahimindro अर्णोव्र्तंarṇovṛtaṃ विvi वर्श्चतvṛścat |

परpra यदyad वयोvayo सsa सवसराण्यछाsvasarāṇyachā परयांसिprayāṃsi चca नदीनांnadīnāṃ चक्रमन्तcakramanta ||
Cheered by this meath Indra, whose hand wields thunder, rent piecemeal Ahi who barred up the waters,
So that the quickening currents of the rivers flowed forth like birds unto their resting-places.
2.19.2
सsa माहिनmāhina इन्द्रोindro अर्णोarṇo अपांapāṃ परैरयदहिहाछाprairayadahihāchā समुद्रमsamudram |

अजनयतajanayat सूर्यंsūryaṃ विददvidad गाghā अक्तुनाह्नांaktunāhnāṃ वयुनानिvayunāni साधतsādhat ||
Indra, this Mighty One, the Dragon's slayer, sent forth the flood of waters to the ocean.
He gave the Sun his life, he found the cattle, and with the night the works of days completed.
2.19.3
सोso अप्रतीनिapratīni मनवेmanave पुरूणीन्द्रोpurūṇīndro दाशदdāśad दाशुषेdāśuṣe हन्तिhanti वर्त्रमvṛtram |

सद्योsadyo योyo नर्भ्योnṛbhyo अतसाय्योatasāyyo भूतbhūt पस्प्र्धानेभ्यःpaspṛdhānebhyaḥ सूर्यस्यsūryasya सातौsātau ||
To him who worshippeth hath Indra given many and matchless gifts. He slayeth Vṛtra.
Straight was he to be sought with supplications by men who struggled to obtain the sunlight.
2.19.4
सsa सुन्वतsunvata इन्द्रःindraḥ सूर्यमाsūryamā देवोdevo रिणंriṇaṃ मर्त्यायmartyāya सतवानstavān |

आā यदyad रयिंrayiṃ गुहदवद्यमस्मैghuhadavadyamasmai भरदंशंbharadaṃśaṃ नैतशोnaitaśo दशस्यनdaśasyan ||
To him who poured him gifts he gave up Sūrya,—Indra, the God, the Mighty, to the mortal;
For Etaśa with worship brought him riches that keep distress afar, as ’twere his portion.
2.19.5
सsa रन्धयतrandhayat सदिवःsadivaḥ सारथयेsārathaye शुष्णमशुषंśuṣṇamaśuṣaṃ कुयवंkuyavaṃ कुत्सायkutsāya |

दिवोदासायdivodāsāya नवतिंnavatiṃ चca नवेन्द्रःnavendraḥ पुरोpuro वयैरच्छम्बरस्यvyairacchambarasya ||
Once to the driver of his chariot, Kutsa, he gave up greedy Sūrya, plague of harvest;
And Indra, for the sake of Divodāsa demolished Śambara's nine-and-ninety castles.
2.19.6
एवाevā तta इन्द्रोचथमहेमindrocathamahema शरवस्याśravasyā नna तमनाtmanā वाजयन्तःvājayantaḥ |

अश्यामaśyāma ततtat साप्तमाशुषाणाsāptamāśuṣāṇā ननमोnanamo वधरदेवस्यvadharadevasya पीयोःpīyoḥ ||
So have we brought our hymn to thee, O Indra, strengthening thee and fain ourselves for glory.
May we with best endeavours gain this friendship, and mayst thou bend the godless scorner's weapons.
2.19.7
एवाevā तेte गर्त्समदाःghṛtsamadāḥ शूरśūra मम्नावस्यवोmamnāvasyavo नna वयुनानिvayunāni तक्षुःtakṣuḥ |

बरह्मण्यन्तbrahmaṇyanta इन्द्रindra तेte नवीयnavīya इषमूर्जंiṣamūrjaṃ सुक्षितिंsukṣitiṃ सुम्नमश्युःsumnamaśyuḥ ||
Thus the Gṛtsamadas for thee, O Hero, have wrought their hymn and task as seeking favour.
May they who worship thee afresh, O Indra, gain food and strength, bliss, and a happy dwelling.
2.19.8
नूनंnūnaṃ साsā ...... ||
Now may that wealthy Cow of thine, O Indra, give in return a boon to him who lauds thee,
Give to thy praisers: let not fortune fail us. Loud may we speak, with heroes, in assembly.
2.19.9
