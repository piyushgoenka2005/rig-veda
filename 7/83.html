HYMN LXXXIII. INDRA-VARUṆA.

युवांyuvāṃ नराnarā पश्यमानासpaśyamānāsa आप्यंāpyaṃ पराचाprācā गव्यन्तःghavyantaḥ पर्थुपर्शवोpṛthuparśavo ययुःyayuḥ |

दासाdāsā चca वर्त्राvṛtrā हतमार्याणिhatamāryāṇi चca सुदासमिन्द्रावरुणावसावतमsudāsamindrāvaruṇāvasāvatam ||
LOOKING to you and your alliance, O ye Men, armed with broad axes they went forward, fain for spoil.
Ye smote and slew his Dāsa and his Āryan enemies, and helped Sudās with favour, Indra-Varuṇa.
7.83.1
यत्राyatrā नरःnaraḥ समयन्तेsamayante कर्तध्वजोkṛtadhvajo यस्मिन्नाजाyasminnājā भवतिbhavati किंचनkiṃcana परियमpriyam |

यत्राyatrā भयन्तेbhayante भुवनाbhuvanā सवर्द्र्शस्तत्राsvardṛśastatrā नna इन्द्रावरुणाधिindrāvaruṇādhi वोचतमvocatam ||
Where heroes come together with their banners raised, in the encounter where is naught for us to love,
Where all things that behold the light are terrified, there did ye comfort us, O Indra-Varuṇa.
7.83.2
संsaṃ भूम्याbhūmyā अन्ताantā धवसिराdhvasirā अद्र्क्षतेन्द्रावरुणाadṛkṣatendrāvaruṇā दिविdivi घोषारुहतghoṣaāruhat |

अस्थुर्जनानामुपasthurjanānāmupa मामरातयो.अर्वागवसाmāmarātayo.arvāghavasā हवनश्रुताhavanaśrutā गतमghatam ||
The boundaries of earth were seen all dark with dust: O Indra-Varuṇa, the shout went up to heaven.
The enmities of the people compassed me about. Ye heard my calling and ye came to me with help.
7.83.3
इन्द्रावरुणाindrāvaruṇā वधनाभिरप्रतिvadhanābhiraprati भेदंbhedaṃ वन्वन्ताvanvantā परpra सुदासमावतमsudāsamāvatam |

बरह्माण्येषांbrahmāṇyeṣāṃ शर्णुतंśṛṇutaṃ हवीमनिhavīmani सत्याsatyā तर्त्सूनामभवतtṛtsūnāmabhavat पुरोहितिःpurohitiḥ ||
With your resistless weapons, Indra-Varuṇa, ye conquered Bheda and ye gave Sudās your aid.
Ye heard the prayers of these amid the cries of war: effectual was the service of the Trtsus' priest.
7.83.4
इन्द्रावरुणावभ्याindrāvaruṇāvabhyā तपन्तिtapanti माघान्यर्योmāghānyaryo वनुषामरातयःvanuṣāmarātayaḥ |

युवंyuvaṃ हिhi वस्वvasva उभयस्यubhayasya राजथो.अधrājatho.adha समाsmā नो.अवतंno.avataṃ पार्येpārye दिविdivi ||
O Indra-Varuṇa, the wickedness of foes and mine assailants' hatred sorely trouble me.
Ye Twain are Lords of riches both of earth and heaven: so grant to us your aid on the decisive day.
7.83.5
युवांyuvāṃ हवन्तhavanta उभयासubhayāsa आजिष्विन्द्रंājiṣvindraṃ चca वस्वोvasvo वरुणंvaruṇaṃ चसातयेcasātaye |

यत्रyatra राजभिर्दशभिर्निबाधितंrājabhirdaśabhirnibādhitaṃ परpra सुदासमावतंsudāsamāvataṃ तर्त्सुभिःtṛtsubhiḥ सहsaha ||
The men of both the hosts invoked you in the fight, Indra and Varuṇa, that they might win the wealth,
What time ye helped Sudās, with all the Trtsu folk, when the Ten Kings had pressed him down in their attack.
7.83.6
दशdaśa राजानःrājānaḥ समिताsamitā अयज्यवःayajyavaḥ सुदासमिन्द्रावरुणाsudāsamindrāvaruṇā नna युयुधुःyuyudhuḥ |

सत्याsatyā नर्णामद्मसदामुपस्तुतिर्देवाnṛṇāmadmasadāmupastutirdevā एषामभवनeṣāmabhavan देवहूतिषुdevahūtiṣu ||
Ten Kings who worshipped not, O Indra-Varuṇa, confederate, in war prevailed not o’er Sudās.
True was the boast of heroes sitting at the feast: so at their invocations Gods were on their side.
7.83.7
दाशराज्ञेdāśarājñe परियत्तायpariyattāya विश्वतःviśvataḥ सुदासsudāsa इन्द्रावरुणावशिक्षतमindrāvaruṇāvaśikṣatam |

शवित्यञ्चोśvityañco यत्रyatra नमसाnamasā कपर्दिनोkapardino धियाdhiyā धीवन्तोdhīvanto असपन्तasapanta तर्त्सवःtṛtsavaḥ ||
O Indra-Varuṇa, ye gave Sudās your aid when the Ten Kings in battle compassed him about,
There where the white-robed Trtsus with their braided hair, skilled in song worshipped you with homage and with hymn.
7.83.8
वर्त्राण्यन्यःvṛtrāṇyanyaḥ समिथेषुsamitheṣu जिघ्नतेjighnate वरतान्यन्योvratānyanyo अभिabhi रक्षतेrakṣate सदाsadā |

हवामहेhavāmahe वांvāṃ वर्षणाvṛṣaṇā सुव्र्क्तिभिरस्मेsuvṛktibhirasme इन्द्रावरुणाindrāvaruṇaā शर्मśarma यछतमyachatam ||
One of you Twain destroys the Vṛtras in the fight, the Other evermore maintains his holy Laws.
We call on you, ye Mighty, with our hymns of praise. Vouchsafe us your protection, Indra-Varuṇa.
7.83.9
अस्मेasme इन्द्रोindro वरुणोvaruṇo मित्रोmitro ...... ||
May Indra, Varuṇa, Mitra, and Aryaman vouchsafe us glory and great shelter spreading far.
We think of the beneficent light of Aditi, and Savitar's song of praise, the God who strengthens Law.
7.83.10
