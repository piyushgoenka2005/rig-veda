HYMN XXV. INDRA.

आā तेte महmaha इन्द्रोतयुग्रindrotyughra समन्यवोsamanyavo यतyat समरन्तsamaranta सेनाःsenāḥ |

पतातिpatāti दिद्युनdidyun नर्यस्यnaryasya बाह्वोर्माbāhvormā तेte मनोmano विष्वद्र्यगviṣvadryagh विvi चारीतcārīt ||
WHEN with thy mighty help, O potent Indra, the armies rush together in their fury.
When from the strong man's arm the lightning flieth, let not the mind go forth to side with others.
7.25.1
निni दुर्गdurgha इन्द्रindra शनथिह्यमित्रानभिśnathihyamitrānabhi येye नोno मर्तासोmartāso अमन्तिamanti |

आरेāre तंtaṃ शंसंśaṃsaṃ कर्णुहिkṛṇuhi निनित्सोराninitsorā नोno भरbhara सम्भरणंsambharaṇaṃ वसूनामvasūnām ||
O Indra, where the ground is hard to traverse, smite down our foes, the mortals who assail us,
Keep far from us the curse of the reviler: bring us accumulated store of treasures.
7.25.2
शतंśataṃ तेte शिप्रिन्नूतयःśiprinnūtayaḥ सुदासेsudāse सहस्रंsahasraṃ शंसाśaṃsā उतuta रातिरस्तुrātirastu |

जहिjahi वधर्वनुषोvadharvanuṣo मर्त्यस्यास्मेmartyasyāsme दयुम्नमधिdyumnamadhi रत्नंratnaṃ चca धेहिdhehi ||
God of the fair helm, give Sudās a hundred succours, a thousand blessings, and thy bounty.
Strike down the weapon of our mortal foeman: bestow upon us splendid fame and riches.
7.25.3
तवावतोtvāvato हीन्द्रhīndra करत्वेkratve अस्मिasmi तवावतो.अवितुःtvāvato.avituḥ शूरśūra रातौrātau |

विश्वेदहानिviśvedahāni तविषीवtaviṣīva उग्रनोकःughranokaḥ कर्णुष्वkṛṇuṣva हरिवोharivo नna मर्धीःmardhīḥ ||
I wait the power of one like thee, O Indra, gifts of a Helper such as thou art, Hero.
Strong, Mighty God, dwell with me now and ever: Lord of Bay Horses, do not thou desert us.
7.25.4
कुत्साkutsā एतेete हर्यश्वायharyaśvāya शूषमिन्द्रेśūṣamindre सहोsaho देवजूतमियानाःdevajūtamiyānāḥ |

सत्राsatrā कर्धिkṛdhi सुहनाsuhanā शूरśūra वर्त्राvṛtrā वयंvayaṃ तरुत्राःtarutrāḥ सनुयामsanuyāma वाजमvājam ||
Here are the Kutsas supplicating Indra for might, the Lord of Bays for God-sent conquest.
Make our foes ever easy to be vanquished: may we, victorious, win the spoil, O Hero.
7.25.5
एवाevā नna इन्द्रindra वार्यस्यvāryasya ...... ||
With precious things, O Indra, thus content us: may we attain to thine exalted favour.
Send our chiefs plenteous food with hero children. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.
7.25.6
