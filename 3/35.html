HYMN XXXV INDRA.

तिष्ठाtiṣṭhā हरीharī रथratha आā युज्यमानाyujyamānā याहिyāhi वायुर्नvāyurna नियुतोniyuto नोno अछacha |

पिबास्यन्धोpibāsyandho अभिस्र्ष्टोabhisṛṣṭo अस्मेasme इन्द्रindra सवाहाsvāhā ररिमातेrarimāte मदायmadāya ||
MOUNT the Bay Horses to thy chariot harnessed, and come to us like Vāyu with his coursers.
Thou, hastening to us, shalt drink the Soma. Hail, Indra. We have poured it for thy rapture.
3.35.1
उपाजिराupājirā पुरुहूतायpuruhūtāya सप्तीsaptī हरीharī रथस्यrathasya धूर्ष्वाdhūrṣvā युनज्मिyunajmi |

दरवदdravad यथाyathā सम्भ्र्तंsambhṛtaṃ विश्वतश्चिदुपेमंviśvataścidupemaṃ यज्ञमावहातyajñamāvahāta इन्द्रमindram ||
For him, the God who is invoked by many, the two swift Bay Steeds to the pole I harness,
That they in fleet course may bring Indra hither, e’en to this sacrifice arranged completely.
3.35.2
उपोupo नयस्वnayasva वर्षणाvṛṣaṇā तपुष्पोतेमवtapuṣpotemava तवंtvaṃ वर्षभvṛṣabha सवधावःsvadhāvaḥ |

गरसेतामश्वाghrasetāmaśvā विvi मुचेहmuceha शोणाśoṇā दिवे-दिवेdive-dive सद्र्शीरद्धिधानाःsadṛśīraddhidhānāḥ ||
Bring the strong Steeds who drink the warm libation, and, Bull of Godlike nature, be thou gracious.
Let thy Steeds eat; set free thy Tawny Horses, and roasted grain like this consume thou daily.
3.35.3
बरह्मणाbrahmaṇā तेte बरह्मयुजाbrahmayujā युनज्मिyunajmi हरीharī सखायाsakhāyā सधमादsadhamāda आशूāśū |

सथिरंsthiraṃ रथंrathaṃ सुखमिन्द्राधितिष्ठनsukhamindrādhitiṣṭhan परजाननprajānan विद्वानुपvidvānupa याहिyāhi सोममsomam ||
Those who are yoked by prayer I harness, fleet friendly Bays who take their joy together.
Mounting thy firm and easy car, O Indra, wise and all-knowing come thou to the Soma.
3.35.4
माmā तेte हरीharī वर्षणाvṛṣaṇā वीतप्र्ष्ठाvītapṛṣṭhā निni रीरमनrīraman यजमानासोyajamānāso अन्येanye |

अत्यायाहिatyāyāhi शश्वतोśaśvato वयंvayaṃ ते.अरंte.araṃ सुतेभिःsutebhiḥ कर्णवामसोमैःkṛṇavāmasomaiḥ ||
No other worshippers must stay beside them thy Bays, thy vigorous and smooth-backed Coursers.
Pass by them all and hasten onward hither: with Soma pressed we will prepare to feast thee.
3.35.5
तवायंtavāyaṃ सोमस्त्वमेह्यर्वांsomastvamehyarvāṃ छश्वत्तमंchaśvattamaṃ सुमनाsumanā अस्यपाहिasyapāhi |

अस्मिनasmin यज्ञेyajñe बर्हिष्याbarhiṣyā निषद्याniṣadyā दधिष्वेमंdadhiṣvemaṃ जठरjaṭhara इन्दुमिन्द्रindumindra ||
Thine is this Soma: hasten to approach it. Drink thou thereof, benevolent, and cease not.
Sit on the sacred grass at this our worship, and take these drops into thy belly, Indra.
3.35.6
सतीर्णंstīrṇaṃ तेte बर्हिःbarhiḥ सुतsuta इन्द्रindra सोमःsomaḥ कर्ताkṛtā धानाdhānā अत्तवेattave तेहरिभ्यामteharibhyām |

तदोकसेtadokase पुरुशाकायpuruśākāya वर्ष्णेvṛṣṇe मरुत्वतेmarutvate तुभ्यंराताtubhyaṃrātā हवींषिhavīṃṣi ||
The grass is strewn for thee, pressed is the Soma; the grain is ready for thy Bays to feed on.
To thee who lovest them, the very mighty, strong, girt by Maruts, are these gifts presented.
3.35.7
इमंimaṃ नरःnaraḥ पर्वतास्तुभ्यमापःparvatāstubhyamāpaḥ समिन्द्रsamindra गोभिर्मधुमन्तमक्रनghobhirmadhumantamakran |

तस्यागत्याtasyāghatyā सुमनाsumanā रष्वṛṣva पाहिpāhi परजाननprajānan विद्वानvidvān पथ्याpathyā अनुanu सवाःsvāḥ ||
This the sweet draught, with cows, the men, the mountains, the waters, Indra, have for thee made ready.
Come, drink thereof, Sublime One, friendly-minded, foreseeing, knowing well the ways thou goest.
3.35.8
यानाभजोyānābhajo मरुतmaruta इन्द्रindra सोमेsome येye तवामवर्धन्नभवनtvāmavardhannabhavan गणस्तेghaṇaste |

तेभिरेतंtebhiretaṃ सजोषाsajoṣā वावशानो.अग्नेःvāvaśāno.aghneḥ पिबpiba जिह्वयासोममिन्द्रjihvayāsomamindra ||
The Maruts, they with whom thou sharedst Soma, Indra, who made thee strong and were thine army,—
With these accordant, eagerly desirous drink thou this Soma with the tongue of Agni.
3.35.9
इन्द्रindra पिबpiba सवधयाsvadhayā चितcit सुतस्याग्नेर्वाsutasyāghnervā पाहिpāhi जिह्वयाjihvayā यजत्रyajatra |

अध्वर्योर्वाadhvaryorvā परयतंprayataṃ शक्रśakra हस्तादhastād धोतुर्वाdhoturvā यज्ञंyajñaṃ हविषोhaviṣo जुषस्वjuṣasva ||
Drink, Indra, of the juice by thine own nature, or by the tongue of Agni, O thou Holy.
Accept the sacrificial gift, O Śakra, from the Adhvaryu's hand or from the Hotar's.
3.35.10
शुनंśunaṃ हुवेमhuvema ...... ||
Call we on Maghavan, auspicious Indra, best Hero in the fight where spoil is gathered,
The Strong, who listens, who.gives aid in battles, who slays the Vṛtras, wins and gathers riches.
