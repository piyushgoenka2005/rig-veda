HYMN XXXVI. INDRA.

इमामूimāmū षुṣu परभ्र्तिंprabhṛtiṃ सातयेsātaye धाःdhāḥ शश्वचःśaśvacḥ छश्वदूतिभिर्यादमानःchaśvadūtibhiryādamānaḥ |

सुते-सुतेsute-sute वाव्र्धेvāvṛdhe वर्धनेभिर्यःvardhanebhiryaḥ कर्मभिर्महद्भिःkarmabhirmahadbhiḥ सुश्रुतोsuśruto भूतbhūt ||
WITH constant succours, fain thyself to share it, make this oblation which we bring effective.
Grown great through strengthening gifts at each libation, he hath become renowned by mighty exploits.
3.36.1
इन्द्रायindrāya सोमाःsomāḥ परदिवोpradivo विदानाvidānā रभुर्येभिर्व्र्षपर्वाṛbhuryebhirvṛṣaparvā विहायाःvihāyāḥ |

परयम्यमानानprayamyamānān परतिprati षूṣū गर्भायेन्द्रghṛbhāyendra पिबpiba वर्षधूतस्यvṛṣadhūtasya वर्ष्णःvṛṣṇaḥ ||
For Indra were the Somas erst- discovered, whereby he grew strong-jointed, vast, and skilful.
Indra , take quickly these presented juices: drink of the strong, that which the strong have shaken.
3.36.2
पिबाpibā वर्धस्वvardhasva तवtava घाghā सुतासsutāsa इन्द्रindra सोमासःsomāsaḥ परथमाprathamā उतेमेuteme |

यथापिबःyathāpibaḥ पूर्व्यानिन्द्रpūrvyānindra सोमानेवाsomānevā पाहिpāhi पन्योpanyo अद्याadyā नवीयानnavīyān ||
Drink and wax great. Thine are the juices, Indra, both Somas of old time and these we bring thee.
Even as thou drankest, Indra, earlier Somas, so drink to-day, a new guest, meet for praises.
3.36.3
महानमत्रोmahānamatro वर्जनेvṛjane विरप्श्युग्रंvirapśyughraṃ शवःśavaḥ पत्यतेpatyate धर्ष्ण्वोजःdhṛṣṇvojaḥ |

नाहnāha विव्याचvivyāca पर्थिवीpṛthivī चनैनंcanainaṃ यतyat सोमासोsomāso हर्यश्वममन्दनharyaśvamamandan ||
Great and impetuous, mighty-voiced in battle, surpassing power is his, and strength resistless.
Him the broad earth hath never comprehended when Somas cheered the Lord of Tawny Coursers.
3.36.4
महानुग्रोmahānughro वाव्र्धेvāvṛdhe वीर्यायvīryāya समाचक्रेsamācakre वर्षभःvṛṣabhaḥ काव्येनkāvyena |

इन्द्रोindro भगोbhagho वाजदाvājadā अस्यasya गावःghāvaḥ परpra जायन्तेjāyante दक्षिणाdakṣiṇā अस्यasya पूर्वीःpūrvīḥ ||
Mighty and strong he waxed for hero exploit: the Bull was furnished a Sage's wisdom.
Indra is our kind Lord; his steers have vigour; his cows are many with abundant offspring.
3.36.5
परpra यतyat सिन्धवःsindhavaḥ परसवंprasavaṃ यथायन्नापःyathāyannāpaḥ समुद्रंsamudraṃ रथ्येवrathyeva जग्मुःjaghmuḥ |

अतश्चिदिन्द्रःataścidindraḥ सदसोsadaso वरीयानvarīyān यदींyadīṃ सोमःsomaḥ पर्णतिpṛṇati दुग्धोdughdho अंशुःaṃśuḥ ||
As floods according to their stream flow onward, so to the sea, as borne on cars, the waters.
Vaster is Indra even than his dwelling, what time the stalk milked out, the Soma, fills him.
3.36.6
समुद्रेणsamudreṇa सिन्धवोsindhavo यादमानाyādamānā इन्द्रायindrāya सोमंsomaṃ सुषुतंsuṣutaṃ भरन्तःbharantaḥ |

अंशुंaṃśuṃ दुहन्तिduhanti हस्तिनोhastino भरित्रैर्मध्वःbharitrairmadhvaḥ पुनन्तिpunanti धारयाdhārayā पवित्रैःpavitraiḥ ||
Eager to mingle with the sea, the rivers carry the well-pressed Soma juice to Indra.
They drain the stalk out with their arms, quick-banded, and cleanse it with a stream of mead and filters.
3.36.7
हरदाhradā इवiva कुक्षयःkukṣayaḥ सोमधानाःsomadhānāḥ समीsamī विव्याचvivyāca सवनाsavanā पुरूणिpurūṇi |

अन्नाannā यदिन्द्रःyadindraḥ परथमाprathamā वयाशvyāśa वर्त्रंvṛtraṃ जघन्वानव्र्णीतjaghanvānavṛṇīta सोममsomam ||
Like lakes appear his flanks filled full with Soma: yea, he contains libations in abundance.
When Indra had consumed the first sweet viands, he, after slaying Vṛtra, claimed the Soma.
3.36.8
आā तूtū भरbhara माकिरेततmākiretat परिpari षठादṣṭhād विद्माvidmā हिhi तवाtvā वसुपतिंvasupatiṃ वसूनामvasūnām |

इन्द्रindra यतyat तेte माहिनंmāhinaṃ दत्रमस्त्यस्मभ्यंdatramastyasmabhyaṃ तदtad धर्यश्वdharyaśva परpra यन्धिyandhi ||
Then bring thou hither, and let none prevent it: we know thee well, the Lord of wealth and treasure.
That splendid gift which is thine own, O Indra, vouchsafe to us, Lord of the Tawny Coursers.
3.36.9
अस्मेasme परpra यन्धिyandhi मघवन्न्र्जीषिन्निन्द्रmaghavannṛjīṣinnindra रायोrāyo विश्ववारस्यviśvavārasya भूरेःbhūreḥ |

अस्मेasme शतंśataṃ शरदोśarado जीवसेjīvase धाdhā अस्मेasme वीरानvīrān छश्वतchaśvata इन्द्रindra शिप्रिनśiprin ||
O Indra, Maghavan, impetuous mover, grant us abundant wealth that brings all blessings.
Give us a hundred autumns for our lifetime: give us, O fair-checked Indra, store of heroes.
3.36.10
शुनंśunaṃ हुवेमhuvema ...... ||
Call we on Indra, Maghavan, auspicious, best Hero in the fight where spoil is gathered,
The Strong, who listens, who gives aid in battles, who slays the Vṛtras, wins and gathers riches.
