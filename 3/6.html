HYMN VI. AGNI.

परpra कारवोkāravo मननाmananā वच्यमानाvacyamānā देवद्रीचींdevadrīcīṃ नयतnayata देवयन्तःdevayantaḥ |

दक्षिणावाडdakṣiṇāvāḍ वाजिनीvājinī पराच्येतिprācyeti हविर्भरन्त्यग्नयेhavirbharantyaghnaye घर्ताचीghṛtācī ||
URGED on by deep devotion, O ye singers, bring, pious ones, the God-approaching ladle.
Borne onward to the right it travels eastward, and, filled with oil, to Agni bears oblation.
3.6.1
आā रोदसीrodasī अप्र्णाapṛṇā जायमानjāyamāna उतuta परpra रिक्थाrikthā अधadha नुnu परयज्योprayajyo |

दिवश्चिदग्नेdivaścidaghne महिनाmahinā पर्थिव्याpṛthivyā वच्यन्तांvacyantāṃ तेte वह्नयःvahnayaḥ सप्तजिह्वाःsaptajihvāḥ ||
Thou at thy birth didst fill both earth and heaven, yea, Most Adorable, thou didst exceed them.
Even through the heaven's and through the earth's expanses let thy swift seventongued flames roll on, O Agni.
3.6.2
दयौश्चdyauśca तवाtvā पर्थिवीpṛthivī यज्ञियासोyajñiyāso निni होतारंhotāraṃ सादयन्तेsādayante दमायdamāya |

यदीyadī विशोviśo मानुषीर्देवयन्तीःmānuṣīrdevayantīḥ परयस्वतीरीळतेprayasvatīrīḷate शुक्रमर्चिःśukramarciḥ ||
Both Heaven and Earth and Gods who should be worshipped establish thee as Priest for every dwelling,
Whenever human families, God-devoted, bringing oblations; laud thy splendid lustre.
3.6.3
महानmahān सधस्थेsadhasthe धरुवdhruva आā निषत्तो.अन्तर्द्यावाniṣatto.antardyāvā माहिनेmāhine हर्यमाणःharyamāṇaḥ |

आस्क्रेāskre सपत्नीsapatnī अजरेajare अम्र्क्तेamṛkte सबर्दुघेsabardughe उरुगायस्यधेनूurughāyasyadhenū ||
Firm in the Gods’ home is the Mighty seated, between vast Heaven and Earth the well-beloved-
Those Cows who yield, unharmed, their nectar, Spouses of the Far-Strider, everyoung, united.
3.6.4
वरताvratā तेte अग्नेaghne महतोmahato महानिmahāni तवtava करत्वाkratvā रोदसीrodasī आā ततन्थtatantha |

तवंtvaṃ दूतोdūto अभवोabhavo जायमानस्त्वंjāyamānastvaṃ नेताnetā वर्षभvṛṣabha चर्षणीनामcarṣaṇīnām ||
Great are the deeds of thee, the Great, O Agni: thou by thy power hast spread out earth and heaven.
As soon as thou wast born thou wast an envoy, thou, Mighty One, was Leader of the people.
3.6.5
रतस्यṛtasya वाvā केशिनाkeśinā योग्याभिर्घ्र्तस्नुवाyoghyābhirghṛtasnuvā रोहिताrohitā धुरिdhuri धिष्वdhiṣva |

अथाathā वहvaha देवानdevān देवdeva विश्वानviśvān सवध्वराsvadhvarā कर्णुहिkṛṇuhi जातवेदःjātavedaḥ ||
Bind to the pole with cords of holy Order the long-maned ruddy steeds who sprinkle fatness.
Bring hithier, O thou God, all Gods together: provide them noble worship, Jātavedas.
3.6.6
दिवश्चिदाdivaścidā तेte रुचयन्तेrucayante रोकाrokā उषोuṣo विभातीरनुvibhātīranu भासिbhāsi पूर्वीःpūrvīḥ |

अपोapo यदग्नyadaghna उशधगuśadhagh वनेषुvaneṣu होतुर्मन्द्रस्यhoturmandrasya पनयन्तpanayanta देवाःdevāḥ ||
Even from the sky thy brilliant lights shone hither: still hast thou beamed through many a radiant morning,
That the Gods praised their joyous Herald's labour eagerly burning, Agni, in the forests.
3.6.7
उरौurau वाvā येye अन्तरिक्षेantarikṣe मदन्तिmadanti दिवोdivo वाvā येye रोचनेrocane सन्तिsanti देवाःdevāḥ |

ऊमाūmā वाvā येye सुहवासोsuhavāso यजत्राyajatrā आयेमिरेāyemire रथ्योrathyo अग्नेaghne अश्वाःaśvāḥ ||
The Gods who take delight in air's wide region, or those the dwellers in heaven's realm of brightness,
Or those, the Holy, prompt to hear, our helpers, who, carborne, turn their horses hither, Agni—-
3.6.8
ऐभिरग्नेaibhiraghne सरथंsarathaṃ याह्यर्वांyāhyarvāṃ नानारथंnānārathaṃ वाvā विभवोvibhavo हयश्वाःhyaśvāḥ |

पत्नीवतस्त्रिंशतंpatnīvatastriṃśataṃ तरींश्चtrīṃśca देवाननुष्वधमाdevānanuṣvadhamā वहvaha मादयस्वmādayasva ||
With these, borne on one ear, Agni, approach us, or borne on many, for thy steeds are able.
Bring, witb their Dames, the Gods, the Three and-Thirty, after thy Godlike nature, and be joyful.
3.6.9
सsa होताhotā यस्यyasya रोदसीrodasī चिदुर्वीcidurvī यज्ञं-यज्ञमभिyajñaṃ-yajñamabhi वर्धेvṛdhe गर्णीतःghṛṇītaḥ |

पराचीprācī अध्वरेवadhvareva तस्थतुःtasthatuḥ सुमेकेsumeke रतावरीṛtāvarī रतजातस्यṛtajātasya सत्येsatye ||
He is the Priest at whose repeated worship even wide Heaven and Earth sing out for increase.
They fair and true and holy coming forward stand at his sacrifice who springs from Order.
3.6.10
इळामग्नेiḷāmaghne ...... ||
As holy food, Agni, to thine invoker give wealth in cattle, lasting, rich in marvels.
To us be born a son and spreading offspring. Agni, be this thy gracious will to usward.
3.6.11
