HYMN CLXVI. MARUTS.

तनtan नुnu वोचामvocāma रभसायrabhasāya जन्मनेjanmane पूर्वंpūrvaṃ महित्वंmahitvaṃ वर्षभस्यकेतवेvṛṣabhasyaketave |

ऐधेवaidheva यामनyāman मरुतस्तुविष्वणोmarutastuviṣvaṇo युधेवyudheva शक्रास्तविषाणिśakrāstaviṣāṇi कर्तनkartana ||
Now let us publish, for the vigorous company the herald of the Strong One, their primeval might.
With fire upon your way, O Maruts loud of voice, with battle, Mighty Ones, achieve your deeds of strength.
1.166.1
नित्यंnityaṃ नna सूनुंsūnuṃ मधुmadhu बिभ्रतbibhrata उपupa करीळन्तिkrīḷanti करीळाkrīḷā विदथेषुvidatheṣu घर्ष्वयःghṛṣvayaḥ |

नक्षन्तिnakṣanti रुद्राrudrā अवसाavasā नमस्विनंnamasvinaṃ नna मर्धन्तिmardhanti सवतवसोsvatavaso हविष्क्र्तमhaviṣkṛtam ||
Bringing the pleasant meath as ’twere their own dear son, they sport in sportive wise gay at their gatherings.
The Rudras come with succour to the worshipper; self-strong they fail not him who offers sacrifice.
1.166.2
यस्माyasmā ऊमासोūmāso अम्र्ताamṛtā अरासतarāsata रायसrāyas पोषंpoṣaṃ चca हविषाhaviṣā ददाशुषेdadāśuṣe |

उक्षन्त्यस्मैukṣantyasmai मरुतोmaruto हिताhitā इवiva पुरूpurū रजांसिrajāṃsi पयसाpayasā मयोभुवःmayobhuvaḥ ||
To whomsoever, bringer of oblations, they immortal guardians, have given plenteous wealth,
For him, like loving friends, the Maruts bringing bliss bedew the regions round with milk abundantly.
1.166.3
आā येye रजांसिrajāṃsi तविषीभिरव्यतtaviṣībhiravyata परpra वva एवासःevāsaḥ सवयतासोध्रजनsvayatāsoadhrajan |

भयन्तेbhayante विश्वाviśvā भुवनानिbhuvanāni हर्म्याharmyā चित्रोcitro वोvo यामःप्रयतास्वyāmaḥprayatāsv रष्टिषुṛṣṭiṣu ||
Ye who with mighty powers have stirred the regions up, your coursers have sped forth directed by themselves.
All creatures of the earth, all dwellings are afraid, for brilliant is your coming with your spears advanced.
1.166.4
यतyat तवेषयामाtveṣayāmā नदयन्तnadayanta पर्वतानparvatān दिवोdivo वाvā पर्ष्ठंpṛṣṭhaṃ नर्याचुच्यवुःnaryāacucyavuḥ |

विश्वोviśvo वोvo अज्मनajman भयतेbhayate वनस्पतीvanaspatī रथीयन्तीवप्रrathīyantīvapra जिहीतjihīta ओषधिःoṣadhiḥ ||
When they in dazzling rush have made the mountains roar, and shaken heaven's high back in their heroic strength,
Each sovran of the forest fears as ye drive near, aid the shrubs fly before you swift as whirling wheels.
1.166.5
यूयंyūyaṃ नna उग्राughrā मरुतःmarutaḥ सुचेतुनारिष्टग्रामाःsucetunāriṣṭaghrāmāḥ सुमतिंsumatiṃ पिपर्तनpipartana |

यत्राyatrā वोvo दिद्युदdidyud रदतिradati करिविर्दतीkrivirdatī रिणातिriṇāti पश्वःpaśvaḥ सुधितेवsudhiteva बर्हणाbarhaṇā ||
Terrible Maruts, ye with ne’er-diminished host, with great benevolence fulfil our heart's desire.
Where’er your lightning bites armed with its gory teeth it crunches up the cattle like a well-aimed dart.
1.166.6
परpra सकम्भदेष्णाskambhadeṣṇā अनवभ्रराधसो.अलात्र्णासोanavabhrarādhaso.alātṛṇāso विदथेषुvidatheṣu सुष्टुताःsuṣṭutāḥ |

अर्चन्त्यर्कंarcantyarkaṃ मदिरस्यmadirasya पीतयेpītaye विदुर्वीरस्यvidurvīrasya परथमानिprathamāni पौंस्याpauṃsyā ||
Givers of during gifts whose bounties never fail, free from ill-will, at sacrifices glorified,
They sing their song aloud that they may drink sweet juice: well do they know the Hero's first heroic deeds.
1.166.7
शतभुजिभिस्तमभिह्रुतेरघातśatabhujibhistamabhihruteraghāt पूर्भीpūrbhī रक्षताrakṣatā मरुतोmaruto यमावतyamāvata |

जनंjanaṃ यमुग्रास्तवसोyamughrāstavaso विरप्शिनःvirapśinaḥ पाथनाpāthanā शंसातśaṃsāt तनयस्यtanayasya पुष्टिषुpuṣṭiṣu ||
With castles hundredfold, O Maruts, guard ye well the man whom ye have loved from ruin and from sin,—
The man whom ye the fierce, the Mighty ones who roar, preserve from calumny by cherishing his seed.
1.166.8
विश्वानिviśvāni भद्राbhadrā मरुतोmaruto रथेषुratheṣu वोvo मिथस्प्र्ध्येवmithaspṛdhyeva तविषाण्याहिताtaviṣāṇyāhitā |

अंसेष्वाaṃseṣvā वःvaḥ परपथेषुprapatheṣu खादयो.अक्षोkhādayo.akṣo वश्चक्राvaścakrā समयाsamayā विvi वाव्र्तेvāvṛte ||
O Maruts, in your cars are all things that are good: great powers are set as ’twere in rivalry therein.
Rings are upon your shoulders when ye journey forth: your axle turns together both the chariot wheels.
1.166.9
भूरीणिbhūrīṇi भद्राbhadrā नर्येषुnaryeṣu बाहुषुbāhuṣu वक्षस्सुvakṣassu रुक्माrukmā रभसासोrabhasāso अञ्जयःañjayaḥ |

अंसेष्वेताःaṃseṣvetāḥ पविषुpaviṣu कषुराkṣurā अधिadhi वयोvayo नna पक्षानpakṣān वयनुvyanu शरियोśriyo धिरेdhire ||
Held in your manly arms are many goodly things, gold chains are on your chests, and glistering ornaments,
Deer-skins are on their shoulders, on their fellies knives: they spread their glory out as birds spread out their wings.
1.166.10
महान्तोmahānto मह्नाmahnā विभ्वोvibhvo विभूतयोvibhūtayo दूरेद्र्शोdūredṛśo येye दिव्याdivyā इवiva सत्र्भिःstṛbhiḥ |

मन्द्राःmandrāḥ सुजिह्वाःsujihvāḥ सवरितारsvaritāra आसभिःāsabhiḥ सम्मिश्लाsammiślā इन्द्रेindre मरुतःmarutaḥ परिष्टुभःpariṣṭubhaḥ ||
Mighty in mightiness, pervading, passing strong, visible from afar as ’twere with stars of heaven,
Lovely with pleasant tongues, sweet singers with their mouths, the Maruts, joined with Indra, shout forth all around.
1.166.11
तदtad वःvaḥ सुजाताsujātā मरुतोmaruto महित्वनंmahitvanaṃ दीर्घंdīrghaṃ वोvo दात्रमदितेरिवdātramaditeriva वरतमvratam |

इन्द्रश्चनindraścana तयजसाtyajasā विvi हरुणातिhruṇāti तज्जनायtajjanāya यस्मैyasmai सुक्र्तेsukṛte अराध्वमarādhvam ||
This is your majesty, ye Maruts nobly born, far as the sway of Aditi your bounty spreads.
Even Indra by desertion never disannuls the boon bestowed by you upon the pious man.
1.166.12
तदtad वोvo जामित्वंjāmitvaṃ मरुतःmarutaḥ परेpare युगेyughe पुरूpurū यच्छंसमम्र्तासावतyacchaṃsamamṛtāsaāvata |

अयाayā धियाdhiyā मनवेmanave शरुष्टिमाव्याśruṣṭimāvyā साकंsākaṃ नरोnaro दंसनैराdaṃsanairā चिकित्रिरेcikitrire ||
This is your kinship, Maruts, that, Immortals, ye were oft in olden time regardful of our call,
Having vouchsafed to man a hearing through this prayer, by wondrous deeds the Heroes have displayed their might.
1.166.13
येनyena दीर्घंdīrghaṃ मरुतःmarutaḥ शूशवामśūśavāma युष्माकेनyuṣmākena परीणसाparīṇasā तुरासःturāsaḥ |

आā यतyat ततननtatanan वर्जनेvṛjane जनासjanāsa एभिर्यज्ञेभिस्तदभीष्टिमश्यामebhiryajñebhistadabhīṣṭimaśyām ||
That, O ye Maruts, we may long time flourish through your abundant riches, O swift movers,
And that our men may spread in the encampment, let me complete the rite with these oblations.
1.166.14
एषeṣa वva सतोमोstomo ...... ||
May this your laud, may this your song, O Maruts, sung by the poet, Māna's son, Māndārya,
Bring offspring for ourselves with food to feed us. May we find strengthening food in full abundance.
1.166.15
