HYMN XCIV. AGNI

इमंimaṃ सतोममर्हतेstomamarhate जातवेदसेjātavedase रथमिवrathamiva संsaṃ महेमाmahemā मनीषयाmanīṣayā |

भद्राbhadrā हिhi नःnaḥ परमतिरस्यpramatirasya संसद्यग्नेsaṃsadyaghne सख्येsakhye माmā रिषामाriṣāmā वयंvayaṃ तवtava ||
FOR Jātavedas worthy of our praise will we frame with our mind this eulogy as ’twere a car.
For good, in his assembly, is this care of ours. Let us not, in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.1
यस्मैyasmai तवमायजसेtvamāyajase सsa साधत्यनर्वाsādhatyanarvā कषेतिkṣeti दधतेdadhate सुवीर्यमsuvīryam |

सsa तूतावtūtāva नैनमश्नोत्यंहतिरग्नेnainamaśnotyaṃhatiraghne ...... ||
The man for whom thou sacrificest prospereth, dwelleth without a foe, gaineth heroic might.
He waxeth strong, distress never approacheth him. Let us not, in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.2
शकेमśakema तवाtvā समिधंsamidhaṃ साधयाsādhayā धियस्त्वेdhiyastve देवाdevā हविरदन्त्याहुतमhaviradantyāhutam |

तवमादित्यानाtvamādityānā वहvaha तानtān हयुश्मस्यग्नेhyuśmasyaghne ...... ||
May we have power to kindle thee. Fulfil our thoughts. In thee the Gods eat the presented offering,
Bring hither the Ādityas, for we long for them. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.3
भरामेध्मंbharāmedhmaṃ कर्णवामाkṛṇavāmā हवींषिhavīṃṣi तेte चितयन्तःcitayantaḥ पर्वणा-पर्वणाparvaṇā-parvaṇā वयमvayam |

जीवातवेjīvātave परतरंprataraṃ साधयाsādhayā धियो.अग्नेdhiyo.aghne ...... ||
We will bring fuel and prepare burnt offerings, reminding thee at each successive festival.
Fulfil our thought that so we may prolong our lives. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.4
विशांviśāṃ गोपाghopā अस्यasya चरन्तिcaranti जन्तवोjantavo दविपच्चdvipacca यदुतyaduta चतुष्पदक्तुभिःcatuṣpadaktubhiḥ |

चित्रःcitraḥ परकेतpraketa उषसोuṣaso महानस्यmahānasya अग्नेaghne ...... ||
His ministers move forth, the guardians of the folk, protecting quadruped and biped with their rays.
Mighty art thou, the wondrous herald of the Dawn. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.5
तवमध्वर्युरुतtvamadhvaryuruta होतासिhotāsi पूर्व्यःpūrvyaḥ परशास्ताpraśāstā पोताpotā जनुषाjanuṣā पुरोहितःpurohitaḥ |

विश्वाviśvā विद्वानार्त्विज्याvidvānārtvijyā धीरdhīra पुष्यस्यग्नेpuṣyasyaghne .... .. ||
Thou art Presenter and the chief Invoker, thou Director, Purifier, great High Priest by birth.
Knowing all priestly work thou perfectest it, Sage. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.6
योyo विश्वतःviśvataḥ सुप्रतीकःsupratīkaḥ सद्रंंsadṛṃṃ असिasi दूरेdūre चितcit सनsan तळिदिवातिtaḷidivāti रोचसेrocase |

रात्र्याश्चिदन्धोrātryāścidandho अतिati देवdeva पश्यस्यग्नेpaśyasyaghne ...... ||
Lovely of form art thou, alike on every side; though far, thou shinest brightly as if close at hand.
O God, thou seest through even the dark of night. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.7
पूर्वोpūrvo देवाdevā भवतुbhavatu सुन्वतोsunvato रथो.अस्माकंratho.asmākaṃ शंसोśaṃso अभ्यस्तुabhyastu दूढ्यःdūḍhyaḥ |

तदाtadā जानीतोतjānītota पुष्यताpuṣyatā वचो.अग्नेvaco.aghne ...... ||
Gods, foremost be his car who pours libations out, and let our hymn prevail o’er evil-hearted men.
Attend to this our speech and make it prosper well. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.8
वधैर्दुःशंसानपvadhairduḥśaṃsānapa दूढ्योdūḍhyo जहिjahi दूरेdūre वाvā येye अन्तिanti वाvā केke चिदत्रिणःcidatriṇaḥ |

अथाathā यज्ञायyajñāya गर्णतेghṛṇate सुगंsughaṃ कर्ध्यग्नेkṛdhyaghne ...... ||
Smite with thy weapons those of evil speech and thought, devouring demons, whether near or tar away.
Then to the singer give free way for sacrifice. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.9
यदयुक्थाyadayukthā अरुषाaruṣā रोहिताrohitā रथेrathe वातजूताvātajūtā वर्षभस्येवvṛṣabhasyeva तेरवःteravaḥ |

आदिन्वसिādinvasi वनिनोvanino धूमकेतुनाग्नेdhūmaketunāghne ...... ||
When to thy chariot thou hadst yoked two red steeds and two ruddy steeds, wind-sped, thy roar was like a bull's.
Thou with smoke-bannered flame attackest forest trees. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.10
अधadha सवनादुतsvanāduta बिभ्युःbibhyuḥ पतत्रिणोpatatriṇo दरप्साdrapsā यतyat तेte यवसादोyavasādo वयस्थिरनvyasthiran |

सुगंsughaṃ ततtat तेte तावकेभ्योtāvakebhyo रथेभ्यो.अग्नेrathebhyo.aghne ...... ||
Then at thy roar the very birds are terrified, when, eating-up the grass, thy sparks fly forth abroad.
Then is it easy for thee and thy car to pass. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.11
अयंayaṃ मित्रस्यmitrasya वरुणस्यvaruṇasya धायसे.अवयातांdhāyase.avayātāṃ मरुतांmarutāṃ हेळोheḷo अद्भुतःadbhutaḥ |

मर्ळाmṛḷā सुsu नोno भूत्वेषांbhūtveṣāṃ मनःmanaḥ पुनरग्नेpunaraghne ...... ||
He hath the Power to soothe Mitra and Varuṇa: wonderful is the Maruts’ wrath when they descend.
Be gracious; let their hearts he turned to us again. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.12
देवोdevo देवानामसिdevānāmasi मित्रोmitro अद्भुतोadbhuto वसुर्वसूनामसिvasurvasūnāmasi चारुरध्वरेcāruradhvare |

शर्मनśarman सयामsyāma तवtava सप्रथस्तमे.अग्नेsaprathastame.aghne ...... ||
Thou art a God, thou art the wondrous Friend of Gods, the Vasu of the Vasus, fair in sacrifice.
Under, thine own most wide protection may we dwell. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.13
ततtat तेte भद्रंbhadraṃ यतyat समिद्धःsamiddhaḥ सवेsve दमेdame सोमाहुतोsomāhuto जरसेjarase मर्ळयत्तमःmṛḷayattamaḥ |

दधासिdadhāsi रत्नंratnaṃ दरविणंdraviṇaṃ चca दाशुषे.अग्नेdāśuṣe.aghne ...... ||
This is thy grace that, kindled in thine own abode, invoked with Soma thou soundest forth most benign,
Thou givest wealth and treasure to the worshipper. Let us not in thy friendship, Agni, suffer harm.
1.94.14
यस्मैyasmai तवंtvaṃ सुद्रविणोsudraviṇo ददाशो.अनागास्त्वमदितेdadāśo.anāghāstvamadite सर्वताताsarvatātā |

यंyaṃ भद्रेणbhadreṇa शवसाśavasā चोदयासिcodayāsi परजावताprajāvatā राधसाrādhasā तेte सयामsyāma ||
To whom thou, Lord of goodly riches, grantest freedom from every sin with perfect wholeness,
Whom with good strength thou
quickenest
, with children and wealth—may we be they, Eternal Being.
1.94.15
सsa तवमग्नेtvamaghne सौभगत्वस्यsaubhaghatvasya विद्वानस्माकमायुःvidvānasmākamāyuḥ परpra तिरेहदेवtirehadeva |

तनtan नोno मित्रोmitro वरुणोvaruṇo मामहन्तामदितिःmāmahantāmaditiḥ सिन्धुःsindhuḥ पर्थिवीpṛthivī उतuta दयौःdyauḥ ||
Such, Agni, thou who knowest all good fortune, God, lengthen here the days of our existence.
This prayer of ours may Varuṇa grant, and Mitra, and Aditi and Sindhu, Earth and Heaven.
