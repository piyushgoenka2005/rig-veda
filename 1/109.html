HYMN CIX. INDRA-AGNI.

विvi हयख्यंhyakhyaṃ मनसाmanasā वस्यvasya इछन्निन्द्राग्नीichannindrāghnī जञासjñāsa उतuta वाvā सजातानsajātān |

नान्याnānyā युवतyuvat परमतिरस्तिpramatirasti मह्यंmahyaṃ सsa वांvāṃ धियंdhiyaṃ वाजयन्तीमतक्षमvājayantīmatakṣam ||
LONGING for weal I looked around, in spirit, for kinsmen, Indra-Agni, or for brothers.
No providence but yours alone is with me so have I wrought for you this hymn for succour.
1.109.1
अश्रवंaśravaṃ हिhi भूरिदावत्तराbhūridāvattarā वांvāṃ विजामातुरुतvijāmāturuta वाvā घाghā सयालातsyālāt |

अथाathā सोमस्यsomasya परयतीprayatī युवभ्यामिन्द्राग्नीyuvabhyāmindrāghnī सतोमंstomaṃ जनयामिjanayāmi नव्यमnavyam ||
For I have heard that ye give wealth more freely than worthless son-in-law or spouse's brother.
So offering to you this draught of Soma, I make you this new hymn, Indra and Agni,
1.109.2
माmā छेद्मchedma रश्मीन्रितिraśmīnriti नाधमानाःnādhamānāḥ पितॄणांpitṝṇāṃ शक्तीरनुयछमानाःśaktīranuyachamānāḥ |

इन्द्राग्निभ्यांindrāghnibhyāṃ कंkaṃ वर्षणोvṛṣaṇo मदन्तिmadanti ताtā हयद्रीhyadrī धिषणायाdhiṣaṇāyā उपस्थेupasthe ||
Let us not break the cords: with this petition we strive to gain the powers of our forefathers.
For Indra-Agni the strong drops are joyful, for here in the bowl's lap are both the press-stones.
1.109.3
युवाभ्यांyuvābhyāṃ देवीdevī धिषणाdhiṣaṇā मदायेन्द्राग्नीmadāyendrāghnī सोममुशतीsomamuśatī सुनोतिsunoti |

तावश्विनाtāvaśvinā भद्रहस्ताbhadrahastā सुपाणीsupāṇī आā धावतंdhāvataṃ मधुनाmadhunā पर्ङकतमप्सुpṛṅktamapsu ||
For you the bowl divine, Indra and Agni, presses the Soma gladly to delight you.
With hands auspicious and fair arms, ye Aśvins, haste, sprinkle it with sweetness in the waters.
1.109.4
युवामिन्द्राग्नीyuvāmindrāghnī वसुनोvasuno विभागेvibhāghe तवस्तमाtavastamā शुश्रवśuśrava वर्त्रहत्येvṛtrahatye |

तावासद्याtāvāsadyā बर्हिषिbarhiṣi यज्ञेyajñe अस्मिनasmin परpra चर्षणीcarṣaṇī मादयेथांmādayethāṃ सुतस्यsutasya ||
You, I have heard, were mightiest, Indra-Agni, when Vṛtra fell and when the spoil was parted.
Sit at this sacrifice, ye ever active, on the strewn grass, and with the juice delight you.
1.109.5
परpra चर्षणिभ्यःcarṣaṇibhyaḥ पर्तनाहवेषुpṛtanāhaveṣu परpra पर्थिव्याpṛthivyā रिरिचाथेriricāthe दिवश्चdivaśca |

परpra सिन्धुभ्यःsindhubhyaḥ परpra गिरिभ्योghiribhyo महित्वाmahitvā परेन्द्राग्नीprendrāghnī विश्वाviśvā भुवनात्यन्याbhuvanātyanyā ||
Surpassing all men where they shout for battle, ye Twain exceed the earth and heaven in greatness.
Greater are ye than rivers and than mountains, O Indra-Agni, and all things beside them.
1.109.6
आā भरतंbharataṃ शिक्षतंśikṣataṃ वज्रबाहूvajrabāhū अस्मानिन्द्राग्नीasmānindrāghnī अवतंavataṃ शचीभिःśacībhiḥ |

इमेime नुnu तेte रश्मयःraśmayaḥ सूर्यस्यsūryasya येभिःyebhiḥ सपित्वंsapitvaṃ पितरोpitaro नna आसनāsan ||
Bring wealth and give it, ye whose arms wield thunder: Indra and Agni, with your powers protect us.
Now of a truth these be the very sunbeams wherewith our fathers were of old united.
1.109.7
पुरन्दराpurandarā शिक्षतंśikṣataṃ वज्रहस्तास्मानिन्द्राग्नीvajrahastāsmānindrāghnī अवतंavataṃ भरेषुbhareṣu |

तनtan नोno ...... ||
Give, ye who shatter forts, whose hands wield thunder: Indra and Agni, save us in our battles.
This prayer of ours may Varuṇa grant, and Mitra, and Aditi and Sindhu, Earth and Heaven.
1.109.8
