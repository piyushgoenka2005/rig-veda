HYMN CIII. INDRA.

ततtat तta इन्द्रियंindriyaṃ परमंparamaṃ पराचैरधारयन्तparācairadhārayanta कवयःkavayaḥ पुरेदमpuredam |

कषमेदमन्यदkṣamedamanyad दिव्यन्यदस्यdivyanyadasya समीsamī पर्च्यतेpṛcyate समनेवsamaneva केतुःketuḥ ||
THAT highest Indra-power of thine is distant: that which is here sages possessed aforetime.
This one is on the earth, in heaven the other, and both unite as flag with flag in battle.
1.103.1
सsa धारयतdhārayat पर्थिवींpṛthivīṃ पप्रथच्चpaprathacca वज्रेणvajreṇa हत्वाhatvā निरपःnirapaḥ ससर्जsasarja |

अहन्नहिमभिनदahannahimabhinad रौहिणंrauhiṇaṃ वयहनvyahan वयंसंvyaṃsaṃ मघवाmaghavā शचीभिःśacībhiḥ ||
He spread the wide earth out and firmly fixed it, smote with his thunderbolt and loosed the waters.
Maghavan with his puissance struck down Ahi, rent Rauhiṇa to death and slaughtered Vyaṁsa.
1.103.2
सsa जातूभर्माjātūbharmā शरद्दधानśraddadhāna ओजःojaḥ पुरोpuro विभिन्दन्नचरदvibhindannacarad विदासीःvidāsīḥ |

विद्वानvidvān वज्रिनvajrin दस्यवेdasyave हेतिमस्यार्यंhetimasyāryaṃ सहोsaho वर्धयाvardhayā दयुम्नमिन्द्रdyumnamindra ||
Armed with his bolt and trusting in his prowess he wandered shattering the forts of Dāsas.
Cast thy dart, knowing, Thunderer, at the Dasyu; increase the Ārya's might and glory, Indra.
1.103.3
तदूचुषेtadūcuṣe मानुषेमाmānuṣemā युगानिyughāni कीर्तेन्यंkīrtenyaṃ मघवाmaghavā नामnāma बिभ्रतbibhrat |

उपप्रयनupaprayan दस्युहत्यायdasyuhatyāya वज्रीvajrī यदyad धdha सूनुःsūnuḥ शरवसेśravase नामnāma दधेdadhe ||
For him who thus hath taught these human races, Maghavan, bearing a fame-worthy title,
Thunderer, drawing nigh to slay the Dasyus, hath given himself the name of Son for glory.
1.103.4
तदस्येदंtadasyedaṃ पश्यताpaśyatā भूरिbhūri पुष्टंpuṣṭaṃ शरदिन्द्रस्यśradindrasya धत्तनdhattana वीर्यायvīryāya |

सsa गाghā अविन्दतavindat सोso अविन्ददश्वानavindadaśvān सsa ओषधीःoṣadhīḥ सोपःsoapaḥ सsa वनानिvanāni ||
See this abundant wealth that he possesses, and put your trust in Indra's hero vigour.
He found the cattle, and he found the horses, he found the plants, the forests and the waters.
1.103.5
भुरिकर्मणेbhurikarmaṇe वर्षभायvṛṣabhāya वर्ष्णेvṛṣṇe सत्यशुष्मायsatyaśuṣmāya सुनवामsunavāma सोममsomam |

यya आद्र्त्याādṛtyā परिपन्थीवparipanthīva शूरो.अयज्वनोśūro.ayajvano विभजन्नेतिvibhajanneti वेदःvedaḥ ||
To him the truly strong, whose deeds are many, to him the strong Bull let us pour the Soma.
The Hero, watching like a thief in ambush, goes parting the possessions of the godless.
1.103.6
तदिन्द्रtadindra परेवpreva वीर्यंvīryaṃ चकर्थcakartha यतyat ससन्तंsasantaṃ वज्रेणाबोधयो.अहिमvajreṇābodhayo.ahim |

अनुanu तवाtvā पत्नीर्ह्र्षितंpatnīrhṛṣitaṃ वयश्चvayaśca विश्वेviśve देवासोdevāso अमदन्ननुamadannanu तवाtvā ||
Well didst thou do that hero deed, O Indra, in waking with thy bolt the slumbering Ahi.
in thee, delighted, Dames divine rejoiced them, the flying Maruts and all Gods were joyful.
1.103.7
शुष्णंśuṣṇaṃ पिप्रुंpipruṃ कुयवंkuyavaṃ वर्त्रमिन्द्रvṛtramindra यदावधीर्विyadāvadhīrvi पुरःशम्बरस्यpuraḥśambarasya |

तनtan नोno ...... ||
As thou hast smitten Śuṣṇa, Pipru, Vṛtra and Kuyava, and Śambara's forts
,
O Indra.
This prayer of ours may Varuṇa grant, and Mitra, and Aditi and Sindhu, Earth and Heaven.
1.103.8
