HYMN CLXXIII. INDRA.

गायतghāyat सामsāma नभन्यंnabhanyaṃ यथाyathā वेरर्चामverarcāma तदtad वाव्र्धानंvāvṛdhānaṃ सवर्वतsvarvat |

गावोghāvo धेनवोdhenavo बर्हिष्यदब्धाbarhiṣyadabdhā आā यतyat सद्मानंsadmānaṃ दिव्यंdivyaṃ विवासानvivāsān ||
THE praise-song let him sing forth bursting bird-like: sing we that hymn which like heaven's light expandeth,
That the milk-giving cows may, unimpeded call to the sacred grass the Gods’ assembly.
1.173.1
अर्चदarcad वर्षाvṛṣā वर्षभिःvṛṣabhiḥ सवेदुहव्यैर्म्र्गोsveduhavyairmṛgho नाश्नोnāśno अतिati यज्जुगुर्यातyajjughuryāt |

परpra मन्दयुर्मनांmandayurmanāṃ गूर्तghūrta होताhotā भरतेbharate मर्योmaryo मिथुनाmithunā यजत्रःyajatraḥ ||
Let the Bull sing with Bulls whose toil is worship, with a loud roar like some wild beast that hungers.
Praised God! the glad priest brings his heart's devotion; the holy youth presents twofold oblation.
1.173.2
नक्षदnakṣad धोताdhotā परिpari सद्मsadma मिताmitā यनyan भरदbharad गर्भमाgharbhamā शरदःśaradaḥ पर्थिव्याःpṛthivyāḥ |

करन्ददश्वोkrandadaśvo नयमानोnayamāno रुवदruvad गौरन्तर्दूतोghaurantardūto नna रोदसीrodasī चरदcarad वाकvāk ||
May the Priest come circling the measured stations, and with him bring the earth's autumnal fruitage.
Let the Horse neigh led near, let the Steer bellow: let the Voice go between both worlds as herald,
1.173.3
ताtā कर्माषतरास्मैkarmāṣatarāsmai परpra चयौत्नानिcyautnāni देवयन्तोdevayanto भरन्तेbharante |

जुजोषदिन्द्रोjujoṣadindro दस्मवर्चाdasmavarcā नासत्येवnāsatyeva सुग्म्योsughmyo रथेष्ठाःratheṣṭhāḥ ||
To him we offer welcomest oblations, the pious bring their strength-inspiring praises.
May Indra, wondrous in his might, accept them, car-borne and swift to move like the Nāsatyas.
1.173.4
तमुtamu षटुहीन्द्रंṣṭuhīndraṃ योyo हha सत्वाsatvā यःyaḥ शूरोśūro मघवाmaghavā योyo रथेष्ठाःratheṣṭhāḥ |

परतीचश्चिदpratīcaścid योधीयानyodhīyān वर्षण्वानvṛṣaṇvān ववव्रुषश्चितvavavruṣaścit तमसोtamaso विहन्ताvihantā ||
Praise thou that Indra who is truly mighty, the car-borne Warrior, Maghavan the Hero;
Stronger in war than those who fight against him, borne by strong steeds, who kills enclosing darkness;
1.173.5
परpra यदित्थाyaditthā महिनाmahinā नर्भ्योnṛbhyo अस्त्यरंastyaraṃ रोदसीrodasī कक्ष्येkakṣye नास्मैnāsmai |

संsaṃ विव्यvivya इन्द्रोindro वर्जनंvṛjanaṃ नna भूमाbhūmā भर्तिbharti सवधावानोपशमिवsvadhāvānopaśamiva दयामdyām ||
Him who surpasses heroes in his greatness: the earth and heavens suffice not for his girdles.
Indra endues the earth to be his garment, and, God-like, wears the heaven as ’twere a frontlet,
1.173.6
समत्सुsamatsu तवाtvā शूरśūra सतामुराणंsatāmurāṇaṃ परपथिन्तमंprapathintamaṃ परितंसयध्यैparitaṃsayadhyai |

सजोषसsajoṣasa इन्द्रंindraṃ मदेmade कषोणीःkṣoṇīḥ सूरिंsūriṃ चिदcid येye अनुमदन्तिanumadanti वाजैःvājaiḥ ||
Thee, Hero, guardian of the brave in battles, who roamest in the van,—to draw thee hither,
Indra, the hosts agree beside the Soma, and joy, for his great actions, in the Chieftain.
1.173.7
एवाevā हिhi तेte शंśaṃ सवनाsavanā समुद्रsamudra आपोāpo यतyat तta आसुāsu मदन्तिmadanti देवीःdevīḥ |

विश्वाviśvā तेte अनुanu जोष्याjoṣyā भूदbhūd गौःghauḥ सूरींश्चिदsūrīṃścid यदिyadi धिषाdhiṣā वेषिveṣi जनानjanān ||
Libations in the sea to thee are pleasant, when thy divine Floods come to cheer these people.
To thee the Cow is sum of all things grateful when with the wish thou seekest men and princes.
1.173.8
असामasāma यथाyathā सुषखायsuṣakhāya एनena सवभिष्टयोsvabhiṣṭayo नरांnarāṃ नna शंसैःśaṃsaiḥ |

असदasad यथाyathā नna इन्द्रोindro वन्दनेष्ठास्तुरोvandaneṣṭhāsturo नna कर्मkarma नयमानnayamāna उक्थाukthā ||
So may we in this One be well befriended, well aided as it were through praise of chieftains,
That Indra still may linger at our worship, as one led swift to work, to hear our praises.
1.173.9
विष्पर्धसोviṣpardhaso नरांnarāṃ नna शंसैरस्माकासदिन्द्रोśaṃsairasmākāsadindro वज्रहस्तःvajrahastaḥ |

मित्रायुवोmitrāyuvo नna पूर्पतिंpūrpatiṃ सुशिष्टौsuśiṣṭau मध्यायुवmadhyāyuva उपupa शिक्षन्तिśikṣanti यज्ञैःyajñaiḥ ||
Like men in rivalry extolling princes, our Friend be Indra, wielder of the thunder.
Like true friends of some city's lord within them held in good rule with sacrifice they help him.
1.173.10
यज्ञोyajño हिhi षमेन्द्रंṣmendraṃ कश्चिदkaścid रन्धञṛndhañ जुहुराणश्चिनjuhurāṇaścin मनसापरियनmanasāpariyan |

तीर्थेtīrthe नाछाnāchā तात्र्षाणमोकोtātṛṣāṇamoko दीर्घोdīrgho नna सिध्रमाsidhramā कर्णोत्यध्वाkṛṇotyadhvā ||
For every sacrifice makes Indra stronger, yea, when he goes around angry in spirit;
As pleasure at the ford invites the thirsty, as the long way brings him who gains his object.
1.173.11
मोmo षूṣū णṇa इन्द्रात्रindrātra पर्त्सुpṛtsu देवैरस्तिdevairasti हिhi षमाṣmā तेte शुष्मिन्नवयाःśuṣminnavayāḥ |

महश्चिदmahaścid यस्यyasya मीळ्हुषोmīḷhuṣo यव्याyavyā हविष्मतोhaviṣmato मरुतोवन्दतेmarutovandate गीःghīḥ ||
Let us not here contend with Gods, O Indra, for here, O Mighty One, is thine own portion,
The Great, whose Friends the bounteous Maruts honour, as with a stream, his song who pours oblations.
1.173.12
एषeṣa सतोमstoma इन्द्रindra तुभ्यमस्मेtubhyamasme एतेनetena गातुंghātuṃ हरिवोharivo विदोvido नःnaḥ |

आā नोno वव्र्त्याःvavṛtyāḥ सुवितायsuvitāya देवdeva विद्यामेषंvidyāmeṣaṃ व.v. ज.j. ||
Addressed to thee is this our praise, O Indra: Lord of Bay Steeds, find us hereby advancement.
So mayst thou lead us on, O God, to comfort. May we find strengthening food in full abundance.
1.173.13
