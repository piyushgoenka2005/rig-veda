HYMN CLXXXII. AŚVINS.

अभूदिदंabhūdidaṃ वयुनमोvayunamo षुṣu भूषताbhūṣatā रथोratho वर्षण्वानvṛṣaṇvān मदताmadatā मनीषिणःmanīṣiṇaḥ |

धियंजिन्वाdhiyaṃjinvā धिष्ण्याdhiṣṇyā विश्पलावसूviśpalāvasū दिवोdivo नपातnapāta सुक्र्तेsukṛte शुचिव्रताśucivratā ||
THIS was the task. Appear promptly, ye prudent Ones. Here is the chariot drawn by strong steeds: be ye glad.
Heart-stirring, longed for, succourers of Viśpalā, here are Heaven's Sons whose sway blesses the pious man.
1.182.1
इन्द्रतमाindratamā हिhi धिष्ण्याdhiṣṇyā मरुत्तमाmaruttamā दस्राdasrā दंसिष्ठाdaṃsiṣṭhā रथ्याrathyā रथीतमाrathītamā |

पूर्णंpūrṇaṃ रथंrathaṃ वहेथेvahethe मध्वmadhva आचितंācitaṃ तेनtena दाश्वांसमुपdāśvāṃsamupa याथोyātho अश्विनाaśvinā ||
Longed for, most Indra-like, mighty, most Marut-like, most wonderful in deed, car-borne, best charioteers,
Bring your full chariot hither heaped with liquid sweet: thereon, ye Aśvins, come to him who offers gifts.
1.182.2
किमत्रkimatra दस्राdasrā कर्णुथःkṛṇuthaḥ किमासाथेkimāsāthe जनोjano यःyaḥ कश्चिदहविर्महीयतेkaścidahavirmahīyate |

अतिati करमिष्टंkramiṣṭaṃ जुरतंjurataṃ पणेरसुंpaṇerasuṃ जयोतिर्विप्रायjyotirviprāya कर्णुतंkṛṇutaṃ वचस्यवेvacasyave ||
What make ye there, ye Mighty? Wherefore linger ye with folk who, offering not, are held in high esteem?
Pass over them; make ye the niggard's life decay: give light unto the singer eloquent in praise.
1.182.3
जम्भयतमभितोjambhayatamabhito रायतःrāyataḥ शुनोśuno हतंhataṃ मर्धोmṛdho विदथुस्तान्यश्विनाvidathustānyaśvinā |

वाचं-वाचंvācaṃ-vācaṃ जरितूjaritū रत्निनींratninīṃ कर्तमुभाkṛtamubhā शंसंśaṃsaṃ नासत्यावतंnāsatyāvataṃ ममmama ||
Crunch up on. every side the dogs who bark at us: slay ye our foes, O Aśvins this ye understand.
Make wealthy every word of him who praises you: accept with favour, both Nāsatyas, this my laud.
1.182.4
युवमेतंyuvametaṃ चक्रथुःcakrathuḥ सिन्धुषुsindhuṣu पलवमात्मन्वन्तंplavamātmanvantaṃ पक्षिणन्तौग्र्यायpakṣiṇantaughryāya कमkam |

येनyena देवत्राdevatrā मनसाmanasā निरूहथुःnirūhathuḥ सुपप्तनीपेतथुःsupaptanīpetathuḥ कषोदसोkṣodaso महःmahaḥ ||
Ye made for Tugra's son amid the water-floods that animated ship with wings to fly withal,
Whereon with God-devoted mind ye brought him forth, and fled with easy flight from out the mighty surge.
1.182.5
अवविद्धंavaviddhaṃ तौग्र्यमप्स्वन्तरनारम्भणेtaughryamapsvantaranārambhaṇe तमसिtamasi परविद्धमpraviddham |

चतस्रोcatasro नावोnāvo जठलस्यjaṭhalasya जुष्टाjuṣṭā उदश्विभ्यामिषिताःudaśvibhyāmiṣitāḥ पारयन्तिpārayanti ||
Four ships most welcome in the midst of ocean, urged by the Aśvins, save the son of Tugra,
Him who was cast down headlong in the waters, plunged in the thick inevitable darkness.
1.182.6
कःkaḥ सविदsvid वर्क्षोvṛkṣo निष्ठितोniṣṭhito मध्येmadhye अर्णसोarṇaso यंyaṃ तौग्र्योtaughryo नाधितःnādhitaḥ पर्यषस्वजतparyaṣasvajat |

पर्णाparṇā मर्गस्यmṛghasya पतरोरिवारभpatarorivārabha उदश्विनाudaśvinā ऊहथुःūhathuḥ शरोमतायśromatāya कमkam ||
What tree was that which stood fixed in surrounding sea to which the son of Tugra supplicating clung?
Like twigs, of which some winged creature may take hold, ye, Aśvins, bore him off safely to your renown.
1.182.7
तदtad वांvāṃ नराnarā नासत्यावनुnāsatyāvanu षयादṣyād यदyad वांvāṃ मानासmānāsa उचथमवोचनucathamavocan |

अस्मादद्यasmādadya सदसःsadasaḥ सोम्यादा]somyādā] वि...vi... ||
Welcome to you be this the hymn of praises uttered by Mānas, O Nāsatyas, Heroes,
From this our gathering where we offer Soma. May we find strengthening food in full abundance.
1.182.8
