HYMN LI. INDRA.

अभिabhi तयंtyaṃ मेषंmeṣaṃ पुरुहूतंpuruhūtaṃ रग्मियमिन्द्रंṛghmiyamindraṃ गीर्भिर्मदताghīrbhirmadatā वस्वोvasvo अर्णवमarṇavam |

यस्यyasya दयावोdyāvo नna विचरन्तिvicaranti मानुषाmānuṣā भुजेbhuje मंहिष्ठमभिmaṃhiṣṭhamabhi विप्रमर्चतvipramarcata ||
MAKE glad with songs that Ram whom many men invoke, worthy of songs of praise, Indra, the sea of wealth;
Whose gracious deeds for men spread like the heavens abroad: sing praise to him the Sage, most liberal for our good.
1.51.1
अभीमवन्वनabhīmavanvan सवभिष्टिमूतयो.अन्तरिक्षप्रांsvabhiṣṭimūtayo.antarikṣaprāṃ तविषीभिराव्र्तमtaviṣībhirāvṛtam |

इन्द्रंindraṃ दक्षासdakṣāsa रभवोṛbhavo मदच्युतंmadacyutaṃ शतक्रतुंśatakratuṃ जवनीjavanī सून्र्तारुहतsūnṛtāruhat ||
As aids the skilful Ṛbhus yearned to Indra strong to save, who fills mid-air, encompassed round with might,
Rushing in rapture; and o’er Śatakratu came the gladdening shout that urged him on to victory.
1.51.2
तवंtvaṃ गोत्रमङगिरोभ्यो.अव्र्णोरपोतात्रयेghotramaṅghirobhyo.avṛṇorapotātraye शतदुरेषुśatadureṣu गातुवितghātuvit |

ससेनsasena चिदcid विमदायावहोvimadāyāvaho वस्वाजावद्रिंvasvājāvadriṃ वावसानस्यनर्तयनvāvasānasyanartayan ||
Thou hast disclosed the kine's stall for the Aṅgirases, and made a way for Atri by a hundred doors.
On Vimada thou hast bestowed both food and wealth, making thy bolt dance in the sacrificer's fight.
1.51.3
तवमपामपिधानाव्र्णोरtvamapāmapidhānāvṛṇor अपाधारयःapādhārayaḥ पर्वतेparvate दानुमदdānumad वसुvasu |

वर्त्रंvṛtraṃ यदिन्द्रyadindra शवसावधीरहिमादितśavasāvadhīrahimādit सूर्यंsūryaṃ दिव्यारोहयोdivyārohayo दर्शेdṛśe ||
Thou hast unclosed the prisons of the waters; thou hast in the mountain seized the treasure rich in gifts.
When thou hadst slain with might the dragon Vṛtra, thou, Indra, didst raise the Sun in heaven for all to see.
1.51.4
तवंtvaṃ मायाभिरपmāyābhirapa मायिनो.अधमःmāyino.adhamaḥ सवधाभिर्येsvadhābhirye अधिadhi शुप्तावजुह्वतśuptāvajuhvata |

तवंtvaṃ पिप्रोर्न्र्मणःpiprornṛmaṇaḥ परारुजःprārujaḥ पुरःpuraḥ परpra रजिश्वानंṛjiśvānaṃ दस्युहत्येष्वाविथdasyuhatyeṣvāvitha ||
With wondrous might thou blewest enchanter fiends away, with powers celestial those who called on thee in jest.
Thou, hero-hearted, hast broken down Pipru's forts, and helped Ṛjiśvan when the Dasyus were struck dead.
1.51.5
तवंtvaṃ कुत्संkutsaṃ शुष्णहत्येष्वाविथारन्धयो.अतिथिग्वायśuṣṇahatyeṣvāvithārandhayo.atithighvāya शम्बरमśambaram |

महान्तंmahāntaṃ चिदर्बुदंcidarbudaṃ निni करमीःkramīḥ पदाpadā सनादेवsanādeva दस्युहत्यायdasyuhatyāya जज्ञिषेjajñiṣe ||
Thou savedst Kutsa when Śuṣṇa was smitten down; to Atithigva gavest Śambara for a prey.
E’en mighty Arbuda thou troddest under foot: thou from of old wast born to strike the Dasyus dead.
1.51.6
तवेtve विश्वाviśvā तविषीtaviṣī सध्र्यगsadhryagh घिताghitā तवtava राधःrādhaḥ सोमपीथायsomapīthāya हर्षतेharṣate |

तवtava वज्रश्चिकितेvajraścikite बाह्वोर्हितोbāhvorhito वर्श्चाvṛścā शत्रोरवśatrorava विश्वानिviśvāni वर्ष्ण्याvṛṣṇyā ||
All power and might is closely gathered up in thee; thy bounteous spirit joys in drinking Soma juice.
Known is the thunderbolt that lies within thine arms: rend off therewith all manly prowess of our foe.
1.51.7
विvi जानीह्यार्यानjānīhyāryān येye चca दस्यवोdasyavo बर्हिष्मतेbarhiṣmate रन्धयाrandhayā शासदव्रतानśāsadavratān |

शाकीśākī भवbhava यजमानस्यyajamānasya चोदिताcoditā विश्वेतviśvet ताtā तेte सधमादेषुsadhamādeṣu चाकनcākana ||
Discern thou well Āryas and Dasyus; punishing the lawless give them up to him whose grass is strewn.
Be thou the sacrificer's strong encourager all these thy deeds are my delight at festivals.
1.51.8
अनुव्रतायanuvratāya रन्धयन्नपव्रतानाभूभिरिन्द्रःrandhayannapavratānābhūbhirindraḥ शनथयन्ननाभुवःśnathayannanābhuvaḥ |

वर्द्धस्यvṛddhasya चिदcid वर्धतोvardhato दयामिनक्षतdyāminakṣata सतवानोstavāno वम्रोvamro विvi जघानjaghāna सन्दिहःsandihaḥ ||
Indra gives up the lawless to the pious man, destroying by the Strong Ones those who have no strength.
Vamra when glorified destroyed the gathered piles of the still waxing great one who would reach the heaven.
1.51.9
तक्षदtakṣad यतyat तta उशनाuśanā सहसाsahasā सहोsaho विvi रोदसीrodasī मज्मनाmajmanā बाधतेbādhate शवःśavaḥ |

आā तवाtvā वातस्यvātasya नर्मणोnṛmaṇo मनोयुजmanoyuja आā पूर्यमाणमवहन्नभिpūryamāṇamavahannabhi शरवःśravaḥ ||
The might which Uśanā hath formed for thee with might rends in its greatness and with strength both worlds apart.
O Hero-souled, the steeds of Vāta, yoked by thought, have carried thee to fame while thou art filled with power.
1.51.10
मन्दिष्टmandiṣṭa यदुशनेyaduśane काव्येkāvye सचानिन्द्रोsacānindro वङकूvaṅkū वङकुतराधिvaṅkutarādhi तिष्ठतिtiṣṭhati |

उग्रोughro ययिंyayiṃ निरपःnirapaḥ सरोतसास्र्जदsrotasāsṛjad विvi शुष्णस्यśuṣṇasya दरंहिताdṛṃhitā ऐरयतairayat पुरःpuraḥ ||
When Indra hath rejoiced with Kāvya Uśanā, he mounts his steeds who swerve wider and wider yet.
The Strong hath loosed his bolt with the swift rush of rain, and he hath rent in pieces Śuṣṇa's firm-built forts.
1.51.11
आā समाsmā रथंrathaṃ वर्षपाणेषुvṛṣapāṇeṣu तिष्ठसिtiṣṭhasi शार्यातस्यśāryātasya परभ्र्तायेषुprabhṛtāyeṣu मन्दसेmandase |

इन्द्रindra यथाyathā सुतसोमेषुsutasomeṣu चाकनो.अनर्वाणंश्लोकमाcākano.anarvāṇaṃślokamā रोहसेrohase दिविdivi ||
Thou mountest on thy car amid strong Soma draughts: Śāryāta brought thee those in which thou hast delight.
Indra, when thou art pleased with men whose Soma flows thou risest to unchallenged glory in the sky.
1.51.12
अददाadadā अर्भांarbhāṃ महतेmahate वचस्यवेvacasyave कक्षीवतेkakṣīvate वर्चयामिन्द्रvṛcayāmindra सुन्वतेsunvate |

मेनाभवोmenābhavo वर्षणश्वस्यvṛṣaṇaśvasya सुक्रतोsukrato विश्वेतviśvet ताtā तेte सवनेषुsavaneṣu परवाच्याpravācyā ||
To old Kakṣīvān, Soma-presser, skilled in song, O Indra, thou didst give the youthful Vṛcayā.
Thou, very wise, wast Menā, Vrsanśva's child: those deeds of thine must all be told at Soma feasts.
1.51.13
इन्द्रोindro अश्रायिaśrāyi सुध्योsudhyo निरेकेnireke पज्रेषुpajreṣu सतोमोstomo दुर्योduryo नna यूपःyūpaḥ |

अश्वयुर्गव्यूaśvayurghavyū रथयुर्वसूयुरिन्द्रrathayurvasūyurindra इदid रायःrāyaḥ कषयतिkṣayati परयन्ताprayantā ||
The good man's refuge in his need is Indra, firm as a doorpost, praised among the Pajras.
Indra alone is Lord of wealth, the Giver, lover of riches, chariots, kine, and horses.
1.51.14
इदंidaṃ नमोnamo वर्षभायvṛṣabhāya सवराजेsvarāje सत्यशुष्मायsatyaśuṣmāya तवसे.अवाचिtavase.avāci |

अस्मिन्निन्द्रasminnindra वर्जनेvṛjane सर्ववीराःsarvavīrāḥ समतsmat सूरिभिस्तवsūribhistava शर्मनśarman सयामsyāma ||
To him the Mighty One, the self-resplendent, verily strong and great, this praise is uttered.
May we and all the heroes, with the princes, be, in this fray, O Indra, in thy keeping.
1.51.15
