HYMN XIX. INDRA.

महानिन्द्रोmahānindro नर्वदाnṛvadā चर्षणिप्राcarṣaṇiprā उतuta दविबर्हाdvibarhā अमिनःaminaḥ सहोभिःsahobhiḥ |

अस्मद्र्यगasmadryagh वाव्र्धेvāvṛdhe विर्यायोरुःviryāyoruḥ पर्थुःpṛthuḥ सुक्र्तःsukṛtaḥ कर्त्र्भिर्भूतkartṛbhirbhūt ||
GREAT, hero-like controlling men is Indra, unwasting in his powers, doubled in vastness.
He, turned to us, hath grown to hero vigour: broad, wide, he hath been decked by those who serve him.
6.19.1
इन्द्रमेवindrameva धिषणाdhiṣaṇā सातयेsātaye धादdhād बर्हन्तंbṛhantaṃ रष्वमजरंṛṣvamajaraṃ युवानमyuvānam |

अषाळ्हेनaṣāḷhena सवसाsavasā शूशुवांसंśūśuvāṃsaṃ सद्यश्चिदsadyaścid योyo वाव्र्धेvāvṛdhe असामिasāmi ||
The bowl made Indra swift to gather booty, the High, the Lofty, Youthful, Undecaying,
Him who hath waxed by strength which none may conquer, and even at once grown to complete perfection.
6.19.2
पर्थूpṛthū करस्नाkarasnā बहुलाbahulā गभस्तीghabhastī अस्मद्र्यकasmadryak संsaṃ मिमीहिmimīhi शरवांसिśravāṃsi |

यूथेवyūtheva पश्वःpaśvaḥ पशुपाpaśupā दमूनाdamūnā अस्मानिन्द्राभ्याasmānindrābhyā वव्र्त्स्वाजौvavṛtsvājau ||
Stretch out those hands of thine, extend to us-ward thy wide capacious arms, and grant us glory.
Like as the household herdsman guards the cattle, so move thou round about us in the combat.
6.19.3
तंtaṃ वva इन्द्रंindraṃ चतिनमस्यcatinamasya शाकैरिहśākairiha नूनंnūnaṃ वाजयन्तोvājayanto हुवेमhuvema |

यथाyathā चितcit पूर्वेpūrve जरितारjaritāra आसुरनेद्याāsuranedyā अनवद्याanavadyā अरिष्टाःariṣṭāḥ ||
Now, fain for strength, let us invite your Indra hither, who lieth hidden with his Heroes,—
Free from all blame, without reproach, uninjured, e’en as were those who sang, of old, his praises.
6.19.4
धर्तव्रतोdhṛtavrato धनदाःdhanadāḥ सोमव्र्द्धःsomavṛddhaḥ सsa हिhi वामस्यvāmasya वसुनःvasunaḥ पुरुक्षुःpurukṣuḥ |

संsaṃ जग्मिरेjaghmire पथ्याpathyā रायोrāyo अस्मिनasmin समुद्रेsamudre नna सिन्धवोsindhavo यादमानाःyādamānāḥ ||
With steadfast laws, wealth-giver, strong through Soma, he hath much fair and precious food to feed us.
In him unite all paths that lead to riches, like rivers that commingle with the ocean.
6.19.5
शविष्ठंśaviṣṭhaṃ नna आā भरbhara शूरśūra शवśava ओजिष्ठमोजोojiṣṭhamojo अभिभूतौग्रमabhibhūtaughram |

विश्वाviśvā दयुम्नाdyumnā वर्ष्ण्याvṛṣṇyā मानुषाणामस्मभ्यंmānuṣāṇāmasmabhyaṃ दाहरिवोdāharivo मादयध्यैmādayadhyai ||
Bring unto us the mightiest might, O Hero, strong and most potent force, thou great Subduer!
All splendid vigorous powers of men vouchsafe us, Lord of Bay Steeds, that they may make us joyful.
6.19.6
यस्तेyaste मदःmadaḥ पर्तणाषाळpṛtaṇāṣāḷ अम्र्ध्रamṛdhra इन्द्रindra तंtaṃ नna आā भरbhara शूशुवांसमśūśuvāṃsam |

येनyena तोकस्यtokasya तनयस्यtanayasya सातौsātau मंसीमहिmaṃsīmahi जिगीवांसस्त्वोताःjighīvāṃsastvotāḥ ||
Bring us, grown mighty in its strength, O Indra, thy friendly rapturous joy that wins the battle,
Wherewith by thee assisted and triumphant, we may laud thee in gaining seed and offspring.
6.19.7
आā नोno भरbhara वर्षणंvṛṣaṇaṃ शुष्ममिन्द्रśuṣmamindra धनस्प्र्तंdhanaspṛtaṃ शूशुवांसंśūśuvāṃsaṃ सुदक्षमsudakṣam |

येनyena वंसामvaṃsāma पर्तनासुpṛtanāsu शत्रूनśatrūn तवोतिभिरुतtavotibhiruta जामीन्रजामीनjāmīnrajāmīn ||
Indra, bestow on us the power heroic skilled and exceeding strong, that wins the booty,
Wherewith, by thine assistance, we may conquer our foes in battle, be they kin or stranger.
6.19.8
आā तेte शुष्मोśuṣmo वर्षभvṛṣabha एतुetu पश्चादोत्तरादधरादाpaścādottarādadharādā पुरस्तातpurastāt |

आā विश्वतोviśvato अभिabhi समेत्वर्वांsametvarvāṃ इन्द्रindra दयुम्नंdyumnaṃ सवर्वदsvarvad धेह्यस्मेdhehyasme ||
Let thine heroic strength come from behind us, before us, from above us or below us.
From every side may it approach us, Indra. Give us the glory of the realm of splendour.
6.19.9
नर्वतnṛvat तta इन्द्रindra नर्तमाभिरूतीnṛtamābhirūtī वंसीमहिvaṃsīmahi वामंvāmaṃ शरोमतेभिःśromatebhiḥ |

ईक्षेīkṣe हिhi वस्वvasva उभयस्यubhayasya राजनrājan धाdhā रत्नंratnaṃ महिmahi सथूरंsthūraṃ बर्हन्तमbṛhantam ||
With most heroic aid from thee, like heroes Indra, may we win wealth by deeds glory.
Thou, King, art Lord of earthly, heavenly treasure: vouchsafe us riches vast, sublime, and lasting.
6.19.10
मरुत्वन्तंmarutvantaṃ वर्षभंvṛṣabhaṃ ...... ||
The Bull, whose strength hath waxed, whom Maruts follow, free-giving Indra, the Celestial Ruler,
Mighty, all-conquering, the victory-giver, him let us call to grant us new protection.
6.19.11
जनंjanaṃ वज्रिनvajrin महिmahi चिनcin मन्यमानमेभ्योmanyamānamebhyo नर्भ्योnṛbhyo रन्धयाrandhayā येष्वस्मिyeṣvasmi |

अधाadhā हिhi तवाtvā पर्थिव्यांpṛthivyāṃ शूरसातौśūrasātau हवामहेhavāmahe तनयेगोष्वप्सुtanayeghoṣvapsu ||
Give up the people who are high and haughty to these men and to me, O Thunder-wielder!
Therefore upon the earth do we invoke thee, where heroes win, for sons and kine and waters.
6.19.12
वयंvayaṃ तta एभिःebhiḥ पुरुहूतpuruhūta सख्यैःsakhyaiḥ शत्रोः-शत्रोरुत्तरśatroḥ-śatroruttara इत्स्यामitsyāma |

घनन्तोghnanto वर्त्राण्युभयानिvṛtrāṇyubhayāni शूरśūra रायाrāyā मदेमmadema बर्हतात्वोताःbṛhatātvotāḥ ||
Through these thy friendships, God invoked of many! may we be victors over every foeman.
Slaying both kinds of foe, may we, O Hero, be happy, helped by thee, with ample riches.
