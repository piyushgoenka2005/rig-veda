HYMN XX. INDRA.

दयौर्नdyaurna यya इन्द्राभिindrābhi भूमार्यस्तस्थौbhūmāryastasthau रयिःrayiḥ शवसाśavasā पर्त्सुpṛtsu जनानjanān |

तंtaṃ नःnaḥ सहस्रभरमुर्वरासांsahasrabharamurvarāsāṃ दद्धिdaddhi सूनोsūno सहसोsahaso वर्त्रतुरमvṛtraturam ||
GIVE us wealth, Indra, that with might, as heaven o’ertops the earth, o’ercomes our foes in battle
Wealth that brings thousands and that wins the corn-lands, wealth, Son of Strength! that vanquishes the foeman.
6.20.1
दिवोdivo नna तुभ्यमन्विन्द्रtubhyamanvindra सत्रासुर्यंsatrāsuryaṃ देवेभिर्धायिdevebhirdhāyi विश्वमviśvam |

अहिंahiṃ यदyad वर्त्रमपोvṛtramapo वव्रिवांसंvavrivāṃsaṃ हन्न्र्जीषिनhannṛjīṣin विष्णुनासचानःviṣṇunāsacānaḥ ||
Even as the power of Dyaus, to thee, O Indra, all Asura sway was by the Gods entrusted,
When thou, Impetuous! leagued with Viṣṇu, slewest Vṛtra the Dragon who enclosed the waters.
6.20.2
तूर्वन्नोजीयानtūrvannojīyān तवसस्तवीयानtavasastavīyān कर्तब्रह्मेन्द्रोkṛtabrahmendro वर्द्धमहाःvṛddhamahāḥ |

राजाभवनrājābhavan मधुनःmadhunaḥ सोम्यस्यsomyasya विश्वासांviśvāsāṃ यतyat पुरांpurāṃ दर्त्नुमावतdartnumāvat ||
Indra, Strong, Victor, Mightier than the mighty, addressed with prayer and perfect in his splendour,
Lord of the bolt that breaketh forts in pieces, became the King of the sweet juice of Soma..
6.20.3
शतैरपद्रनśatairapadran पणयpaṇaya इन्द्रात्रindrātra दशोणयेdaśoṇaye कवये.अर्कसातौkavaye.arkasātau |

वधैःvadhaiḥ शुष्णस्याशुषस्यśuṣṇasyāśuṣasya मायाःmāyāḥ पित्वोpitvo नारिरेचीतnārirecīt किंचनkiṃcana परpra ||
There, Indra, while the light was won, the Paṇis f1ed, 'neath a hundred blows, for wise Dasoni,
And greedy Śuṣṇa's magical devices nor left he any of their food remaining.
6.20.4
महोmaho दरुहोdruho अपapa विश्वायुviśvāyu धायिdhāyi वज्रस्यvajrasya यतyat पतनेpatane पादिpādi शुष्णःśuṣṇaḥ |

उरुuru षṣa सरथंsarathaṃ सारथयेsārathaye करिन्द्रःkarindraḥ कुत्सायkutsāya सूर्यस्यsūryasya सातौsātau ||
What time the thunder fell and Śuṣṇa perished, all life's support from the great Druh was taken.
Indra made room for his car-drivcr Kutsa who sate beside him, when he gained the sunlight.
6.20.5
परpra शयेनोśyeno नna मदिरमंशुमस्मैmadiramaṃśumasmai शिरोśiro दासस्यdāsasya नमुचेर्मथायनnamucermathāyan |

परावनprāvan नमींnamīṃ साप्यंsāpyaṃ ससन्तंsasantaṃ पर्णगpṛṇagh रायाrāyā समिषाsamiṣā संsaṃ सवस्तिsvasti ||
As the Hawk rent for him the stalk that gladdens, he wrenched the head from Namuci the Dāsa.
He guarded Nam, Sayya's son, in slumber, and sated him with food, success, and riches.
6.20.6
विvi पिप्रोरहिमायस्यpiprorahimāyasya दर्ळ्हाःdṛḷhāḥ पुरोpuro वज्रिञ्छवसाvajriñchavasā नna दर्दःdardaḥ |

सुदामनsudāman तदtad रेक्णोrekṇo अप्रम्र्ष्यंapramṛṣyaṃ रजिश्वनेṛjiśvane दात्रंdātraṃ दाशुषेdāśuṣe दाःdāḥ ||
Thou, thunder-armed, with thy great might hast shattered Pipru's strong forts who knew the wiles of serpents.
Thou gavest to thy worshipper Ṛjiśvan imperishable Wealth, O Bounteous Giver.
6.20.7
सsa वेतसुंvetasuṃ दशमायंdaśamāyaṃ दशोणिंdaśoṇiṃ तूतुजिमिन्द्रःtūtujimindraḥ सवभिष्टिसुम्नःsvabhiṣṭisumnaḥ |

आā तुग्रंtughraṃ शश्वदिभंśaśvadibhaṃ दयोतनायdyotanāya मातुर्नmāturna सीमुपsīmupa सर्जाsṛjā इयध्यैiyadhyai ||
The crafty Vetasu, the swift Dasni, and Tugra speedily with all his servants,
Hath Indra, gladdening with strong assistance, forced near as ’twere to glorify the Mother.
6.20.8
सsa ईंīṃ सप्र्धोspṛdho वनतेvanate अप्रतीतोapratīto बिभ्रदbibhrad वज्रंvajraṃ वर्त्रहणंvṛtrahaṇaṃ गभस्तौghabhastau |

तिष्ठदtiṣṭhad धरीdharī अध्यस्तेवadhyasteva गर्तेgharte वचोयुजाvacoyujā वहतvahata इन्द्रंindraṃ रष्वमṛṣvam ||
Resistless, with the hosts he battles, bearing in both his arms the Vṛtra-slaying thunder.
He mounts his Bays, as the car-seat an archer: yoked at a word they bear the lofty Indra.
6.20.9
सनेमsanema ते.अवसाte.avasā नव्यnavya इन्द्रindra परpra पूरवpūrava सतवन्तstavanta एनाenā यज्ञैःyajñaiḥ |

सप्तsapta यतyat पुरःpuraḥ शर्मśarma शारदीर्दर्दśāradīrdard धनdhan दासीःdāsīḥ पुरुकुत्सायpurukutsāya शिक्षनśikṣan ||
May we, O Indra, gain by thy new favour: so Parus laud thee, with their sacrifices,
That thou hast wrecked seven autumn forts, their shelter, slain Dāsa tribes and aided Purukutsa.
6.20.10
तवंtvaṃ वर्धvṛdha इन्द्रindra पूर्व्योpūrvyo भूर्वरिवस्यन्नुशनेbhūrvarivasyannuśane काव्यायkāvyāya |

पराparā नववास्त्वमनुदेयंnavavāstvamanudeyaṃ महेmahe पित्रेpitre ददाथdadātha सवंsvaṃ नपातमnapātam ||
Favouring Uśanā the son of Kavi, thou wast his ancient strengthener, O Indra.
Thou gavest Navavāstva. as a present, to the great father gavest back his grandson.
6.20.11
तवंtvaṃ धुनिरिन्द्रdhunirindra ...... ||
Thou, roaring Indra, drovest on the waters that made a roaring sound like rushing rivers,
What time, O Hero, o’er the sea thou broughtest, in safety broughtest Turvaśa and Yadu.
6.20.12
तवtava हha तयदिन्द्रtyadindra विष्वमाजौviṣvamājau सस्तोsasto धुनीचुमुरीdhunīcumurī याyā हha सिष्वपsiṣvap |

दीदयदितdīdayadit तुभ्यंtubhyaṃ सोमेभिःsomebhiḥ सुन्वनsunvan दभीतिरिध्मभ्र्तिःdabhītiridhmabhṛtiḥ पक्थ्यर्कैःpakthyarkaiḥ ||
This Indra, was thy work in war: thou sentest Dhuni and Cumuri to sleep and slumber.
Dabhīti lit the flame for thee, and worshipped with fuel, hymns, poured Soma, dressed oblations.
