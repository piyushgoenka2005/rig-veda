HYMN LXXXI. SOMA PAVAMANA.

परpra सोमस्यsomasya पवमानस्योर्मयpavamānasyormaya इन्द्रस्यindrasya यन्तिyanti जठरंjaṭharaṃ सुपेशसःsupeśasaḥ |

दध्नाdadhnā यदीमुन्नीताyadīmunnītā यशसाyaśasā गवांghavāṃ दानायdānāya शूरमुदमन्दिषुःśūramudamandiṣuḥ सुताःsutāḥ ||
ONWARD to Indra's throat move, beauteously adorned, the waves of Soma as he purifies himself,
When they, brought forward with the lovely curd of kine, effused, have cheered the Hero to bestow his gifts.
9.81.1
अछाachā हिhi सोमःsomaḥ कलशानसिष्यददत्योkalaśānasiṣyadadatyo नna वोळ्हाvoḷhā रघुवर्तनिर्व्र्षाraghuvartanirvṛṣā |

अथाathā देवानामुभयस्यdevānāmubhayasya जन्मनोjanmano विद्वानश्नोत्यमुतvidvānaśnotyamuta इतश्चitaśca यतyat ||
Hither hath Soma flowed unto the beakers, like a chariot-horse, a stallion swift upon his way.
Thus, knowing both the generations, he obtains the rights and dues of Gods from yonder and from hence.
9.81.2
आā नःnaḥ सोमsoma पवमानःpavamānaḥ किराkirā वस्विन्दोvasvindo भवbhava मघवाmaghavā राधसोrādhaso महःmahaḥ |

शिक्षाśikṣā वयोधोvayodho वसवेvasave सुsu चेतुनाcetunā माmā नोno गयमारेghayamāre अस्मतasmat पराparā सिचःsicaḥ ||
While thou art cleansed, O Soma, scatter wealth on us; Indu, bestow great bounty as a liberal Prince.
Giver of life, with wisdom help to opulence; strew not our home possessions far away from us.
9.81.3
आā नःnaḥ पूषाpūṣā पवमानःpavamānaḥ सुरातयोsurātayo मित्रोmitro गछन्तुghachantu वरुणःvaruṇaḥ सजोषसःsajoṣasaḥ |

बर्हस्पतिर्मरुतोbṛhaspatirmaruto वायुरश्विनाvāyuraśvinā तवष्टाtvaṣṭā सविताsavitā सुयमाsuyamā सरस्वतीsarasvatī ||
Hither let Pūṣan Pavamana come to us, Varuṇa, Mitra, bountiful, of one accord,
The Maruts, Aśvins, Vāyu, and Bṛhaspati, Savitar, Tvaṣṭar, tractable Sarasvatī.
9.81.4
उभेubhe दयावाप्र्थिवीdyāvāpṛthivī विश्वमिन्वेviśvaminve अर्यमाaryamā देवोdevo अदितिर्विधाताaditirvidhātā |

भगोbhagho नर्शंसnṛśaṃsa उर्वन्तरिक्षंurvantarikṣaṃ विश्वेviśve देवाःdevāḥ पवमानंजुषन्तpavamānaṃjuṣanta ||
Both Heaven and Earth, the all-invigorating Pair, Vidhatar, Aditi, and Aryaman the God,
Bhaga who blesses men, the spacious Firmament,—let all the Gods in Pavamana take delight.
